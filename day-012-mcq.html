Day 12: Risk Response Strategies – Practice Quiz

Domain 1 – Security and Risk Management
📝 10 Questions🎯 CISSP Level✅ Pass: 7/10

1A risk analysis determines that a legacy system poses a $50,000 ALE. Implementing the only available mitigating control would cost $500,000. The system cannot be decommissioned because it supports a critical business function. What is the MOST appropriate risk response?
✅ Correct: C. When a control cost (10×) far exceeds the ALE, and the risk cannot be avoided (business-critical system), formal Risk Acceptance is appropriate. Acceptance is NOT passive inaction — it is a documented, executive-authorized decision to tolerate the risk level. The acceptance should be: documented in the risk register, reviewed periodically, approved by an appropriate authority, and accompanied by monitoring. Option A (transfer) is also valid but cyber insurance premiums for a $50K ALE risk would likely approach the expected loss anyway.
2A company purchases a $5M cyber liability insurance policy covering breach costs including incident response, breach notification, legal fees, and regulatory fines. Following a breach costing $4.2M, which statement MOST accurately describes the limitations of this risk transfer strategy?
✅ Correct: B. Risk transfer (insurance) shares financial consequences with a third party but does NOT eliminate risk. Key limitations: deductibles reduce recovery; sub-limits may cap specific categories (e.g., ransomware sublimit); policy exclusions may deny certain claims (war, nation-state attacks); reputational damage, customer attrition, employee morale impacts, and regulatory compliance failures are not covered by insurance. Insurance is a supplement to, not a replacement for, preventive controls. CISSP candidates must articulate what risk transfer does and does NOT address.
3An organisation's e-commerce platform processes credit card payments. After a risk assessment, the CISO recommends replacing direct card number storage with a tokenisation service where the organisation never handles raw PANs. Which risk response strategy does this MOST closely represent?
✅ Correct: C. Risk Avoidance eliminates the risk by eliminating the activity or process that creates the risk. Tokenisation removes the organisation from the scope of PCI-DSS cardholder data environment — they never receive or store raw PANs, so the risk of cardholder data theft is avoided. This is different from Mitigation (reducing the likelihood/impact of a risk that still exists) and Transfer (shifting financial consequences while the risk remains). Avoidance is the most complete risk response but often involves business trade-offs.
4An organisation is subject to a regulatory requirement to log all privileged user activity. The SIEM required to fulfil this is expensive and technically complex to deploy on one legacy system. The security team proposes enhanced manual review procedures as an alternative. Under risk management, this enhanced manual review is classified as:
✅ Correct: D. A Compensating Control provides equivalent or acceptable risk reduction when the standard control cannot be implemented due to technical, operational, or financial constraints. PCI-DSS, HIPAA, and other frameworks formally recognise compensating controls — they require documented justification proving the alternative achieves the same control objective. Compensating controls are not permanent replacements; they should be reviewed regularly and replaced with primary controls when feasible. This is different from risk acceptance — compensating controls still address the risk; they just do so differently.
5A "Beware of Dog" sign posted at a facility entrance is intended to make potential intruders think twice before attempting to break in. Whether or not a dog is present, the sign attempts to reduce intrusion attempts. This is an example of which type of security control?
✅ Correct: C. A Deterrent control discourages malicious acts by increasing the perceived cost, detection risk, or effort for the attacker — without necessarily stopping a determined attacker. Examples: warning banners ("this system is monitored"), security cameras (whether monitored or not), security guard presence. A Preventive control actually stops the attack (e.g., locked door). A Detective control identifies that an attack occurred (e.g., alarm triggered). Distinguishing deterrent from preventive is a frequent CISSP trap — deterrents influence perception/psychology; preventives impose physical/logical barriers.
6A security operations team reviews authentication logs daily looking for anomalous login patterns — multiple failed attempts followed by success from unusual geographic locations. This control is BEST classified as:
✅ Correct: B. Log review for anomalous patterns is a Detective control — it identifies that a suspicious event has occurred or is occurring, triggering investigation and response. The control category is Technical (automated log collection, correlation) and the function is Detective (awareness, not prevention). Controls are classified by both FUNCTION (preventive, detective, corrective, deterrent, recovery, compensating) and TYPE (administrative/management, technical/logical, physical/operational). Reviewing logs does not prevent the login attempt — it detects it after the fact.
7After all controls are implemented, a risk register shows $150,000 in residual risk across several categories. The risk committee proposes formally accepting this residual risk. Who is the MOST appropriate party to sign off on formal risk acceptance?
✅ Correct: C. Risk acceptance must be authorized by the appropriate risk owner — the business executive or management authority accountable for the business processes creating the risk. The CISO can recommend but should not accept risk on behalf of the business — the CISO does not own the business consequences of a loss event. The analyst (B) has no authority to accept risk. The auditor (D) assesses controls but does not make risk treatment decisions. This is the CISSP "manager mindset": accountability and authority must align in risk acceptance decisions.
8An organisation implements a policy requiring that all wire transfers exceeding $50,000 must be initiated by one financial analyst and independently approved by a different senior manager. This control is BEST classified as:
✅ Correct: B. Separation of Duties (SoD) — requiring two different people to complete a sensitive transaction — is a Preventive, Administrative control. It prevents fraud by ensuring no individual can both initiate and approve a transaction. "Administrative" refers to the control type: it's implemented through policy, procedure, and role design rather than technology or physical barriers. SoD is one of the most important fraud prevention controls in financial systems. The "four-eyes principle" is the common name for this two-person approval requirement.
9A hospital identifies that its end-of-life radiology systems run on Windows XP and cannot be patched. The vendor provides no security updates. The hospital applies network segmentation and host-based IDS around these systems as interim measures while planning migration. These measures represent which risk response?
✅ Correct: C. Risk Mitigation reduces the likelihood, impact, or both. Network segmentation reduces the probability that an attacker can reach the unpatched systems from the broader network (likelihood reduction). Host-based IDS increases detection probability, enabling faster response to contain damage (impact reduction). These are compensating controls applied in mitigation mode. The migration plan represents future avoidance — but until complete, mitigation is the active response. This scenario is extremely common in healthcare and OT environments where legacy systems cannot be immediately replaced.
10A security manager recommends against deploying a DLP solution because quantitative analysis shows a negative ROI. However, the legal team advises that a specific regulation requires DLP-equivalent controls for the organisation's data categories. How should this conflict be resolved?
✅ Correct: C. Mandatory regulatory controls cannot be declined based on negative ROI — legal and regulatory obligations are constraints within which risk management operates, not options subject to cost-benefit analysis. The consequences of non-compliance (fines, regulatory sanctions, criminal liability, licence revocation, reputational damage) invariably dwarf the control implementation cost when assessed holistically. Risk acceptance is not appropriate for legally mandated controls. This is a critical CISSP principle: business decisions operate within legal and regulatory constraints — they do not override them.
