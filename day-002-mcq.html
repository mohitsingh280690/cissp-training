Day 2: Security Governance – Practice Quiz

Domain 1 – Security and Risk Management
📝 10 Questions🎯 CISSP Level✅ Pass: 7/10

1A newly hired database administrator is instructed by the security team to encrypt a sensitive HR database. Who is MOST appropriately responsible for determining the HR data's classification level and authorizing the encryption requirement?
✅ Correct: C. The Data Owner is the business manager or executive with operational responsibility for the data — in this case, the HR Department Manager. Data Owners are responsible for classifying data and determining access and protection requirements. The DBA is a Data Custodian (implements technical controls at the Owner's direction). The CISO sets policy; the IT Director may be the System Owner for the underlying platform, but neither owns the HR data itself.
2Which statement BEST describes the distinction between security governance and security management?
✅ Correct: C. Governance is the board/executive function — setting direction, ensuring accountability, and overseeing strategy. Management translates governance direction into operational programs and activities. Option A inverts the relationship. Option D has the roles reversed. A common exam trap is conflating governance (strategic oversight) with operational management.
3An organization researches industry best practices, documents its security decisions, and implements appropriate controls. A breach still occurs. Which concept BEST describes the legal position these actions support?
✅ Correct: B. Due diligence = researching and understanding what security measures are appropriate (doing your homework). Due care = actually implementing those measures (doing the right thing). Both together form a defensible legal posture by demonstrating the organization acted reasonably. Option D is wrong because the presence of a breach doesn't by itself mean due care was absent — no controls can guarantee zero breaches.
4A company is acquiring a smaller firm. From a security governance perspective, what should be the FIRST action taken regarding the acquired company's security posture?
✅ Correct: B. Assess before integrating. Connecting an unknown network to your own or instantly applying policies without understanding the existing state could introduce serious vulnerabilities or create conflicts. The CISSP principle is to understand the risk landscape first. NIST and governance frameworks consistently prescribe assessment as the first step in any acquisition security integration.
5Which of the following activities is MOST appropriately the responsibility of the CISO?
✅ Correct: C. The CISO is a senior management role focused on strategy, governance, and risk — not technical implementation. Firewall configuration belongs to security engineers/administrators. Forensics belongs to the incident response team. While the CISO may influence vendor selection, personally negotiating individual licenses is an operational procurement activity. The exam expects "manager mindset" — the CISO defines the what and why; others execute the how.
6A single system administrator both creates user accounts AND approves access requests for the same systems. Which security principle is being violated?
✅ Correct: C. Separation of Duties (SoD) requires that sensitive tasks be divided among multiple individuals to prevent fraud and error. When one person can both create accounts and approve them, they can grant themselves or others unauthorized access without a second check. While Least Privilege is related, the specific principle violated here is SoD — the structural check-and-balance between creation and approval.
7A CISO presents a security roadmap to the board. Directors ask how each initiative ties to business objectives. Which framework BEST supports demonstrating alignment between information security governance and business strategy?
✅ Correct: B. COBIT is specifically designed for IT governance and aligning IT/security investments with business objectives — it speaks in business language to non-technical boards. NIST SP 800-53 is a comprehensive technical control catalog. ISO 27001 provides an ISMS framework. PCI-DSS is a compliance standard for payment cards. When the question is about business value alignment and governance, COBIT is the correct answer.
8An auditor discovers that a critical security decision affecting customer data was implemented by an IT team with no documented approval from senior management. Which governance element is most directly absent?
✅ Correct: B. Accountability requires that decisions be traceable to specific individuals who have the authority and responsibility for those decisions. Without documented management approval, no one is formally accountable. While a risk assessment may also be absent, the core governance failure when decisions lack documented authority is accountability — a foundational governance requirement.
9Following a merger, the acquired company classified customer data as "Internal Use Only." The acquiring company's policy classifies equivalent data as "Confidential." What is the MOST appropriate governance action during integration?
✅ Correct: C. Data Owners are responsible for classification decisions. Reclassification without business owner review could result in incorrect access controls. Defaulting to the lower classification is a security risk. A governed reconciliation process — led by the appropriate Data Owners — ensures the right classification decisions are made with the right accountability. Automatic or IT-driven reclassification bypasses governance.
10The Board of Directors asks the CISO for the organization's current information security risk profile. Which document would BEST satisfy this governance reporting need?
✅ Correct: C. The board needs information at the governance level — business risk expressed in terms of impact, likelihood, and treatment strategy. Technical artifacts (CVE reports, firewall rules, helpdesk tickets) are operational and not meaningful at board level. An executive risk register translates security risk into business language, enabling informed governance decisions about risk appetite and resource allocation.
