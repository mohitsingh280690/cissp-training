2. Core Concepts: Governance, Compliance & Frameworks

Policy Hierarchy

Policy → Standard → Guideline → Procedure:
Policy = High-level management intent and direction (WHY and WHAT)
Standard = Mandatory, specific, measurable requirements (WHAT exactly is required)
Guideline = Recommended, flexible practices (non-mandatory HOW suggestions)
Procedure = Step-by-step instructions (HOW to perform a specific task)

Due Care vs. Due Diligence

TermDefinitionMemory Aid
Due CareDoing what a prudent person would reasonably do to protect assets; the standard of care actually implementedCare = ACT (doing something)
Due DiligenceResearching, investigating, and continually monitoring to understand what needs to be done to exercise due careDiligence = RESEARCH (knowing what to do)

3. Risk Management Fundamentals

Risk Responses — MATA

ResponseActionExample
TransferShift risk to a third partyCyber liability insurance; outsourcing risk
AvoidEliminate the risky activity entirelyStop storing credit card numbers; exit a high-risk market
MitigateReduce likelihood or impact through controlsApply patches; implement MFA; deploy encryption
AcceptAcknowledge the risk and bear the consequencesAccept residual risk after controls; document decision
⚠️ Remember: "Ignore" is not a valid risk response. Accepting risk is a conscious, documented decision made by management. Ignoring risk is a failure of governance. The CISSP exam will not accept "ignore" as an answer — the closest valid response is "accept" (with documented management sign-off).

Risk Types

  • Inherent Risk: Risk before any controls are applied
  • Residual Risk: Risk remaining after controls are applied
  • Total Risk: Risk if no controls existed at all (theoretical maximum)
  • Risk Appetite: How much risk the organisation is willing to accept strategically
  • Risk Tolerance: Acceptable variation around risk appetite targets

4. Key Formulas You Must Know

ALE = SLE × ARO

ALE = Annualised Loss Expectancy (annual risk cost)
SLE = Single Loss Expectancy = Asset Value (AV) × Exposure Factor (EF)
ARO = Annualised Rate of Occurrence (how often the threat happens per year)

SLE = AV × EF

AV = Asset Value; EF = Exposure Factor (percentage of asset lost per incident, 0.0–1.0)

ROSI = (ALE_before − ALE_after) − Cost_of_Control

Return on Security Investment — the annual net benefit of implementing a control

RTO + WRT ≤ MTD

RTO = Recovery Time Objective (time to restore systems after incident)
WRT = Work Recovery Time (time to restore data/backlogs after systems return)
MTD = Maximum Tolerable Downtime (maximum survivable outage)

Availability = MTBF ÷ (MTBF + MTTR)

MTBF = Mean Time Between Failures (average reliability interval)
MTTR = Mean Time To Repair (average time to restore after failure)

✅ Exam Tip — Formula Application: Server AV = $100,000; EF = 40%; ARO = 0.5/year
SLE = $100,000 × 0.40 = $40,000
ALE = $40,000 × 0.5 = $20,000 per year
If a control costs $8,000/year and reduces ARO to 0.1: New ALE = $40,000 × 0.1 = $4,000
ROSI = ($20,000 − $4,000) − $8,000 = $8,000/year benefit

7. Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery

BCP vs. DRP

BCP (Business Continuity Plan): Strategic-level plan for how the organisation continues business operations during a disruption. Focuses on business functions, not just IT. Longer-term.

DRP (Disaster Recovery Plan): Tactical-level plan for how IT systems are recovered after a disaster. Subset of BCP. More technical, shorter-term recovery focus.

9. Common CISSP Traps in Domain 1

⚠️ Trap 1 — Ignore ≠ Accept: "Ignore" is not a valid risk response. The correct term is "Accept" — a documented management decision to bear the risk. Don't confuse the two.
⚠️ Trap 2 — Guideline vs. Standard: Guidelines are optional recommendations; Standards are mandatory requirements. "Best practice guidance documents" are guidelines; "You MUST use AES-256" is a standard.
⚠️ Trap 3 — Due Care vs. Due Diligence: Due Care = acting (implementing controls); Due Diligence = researching and monitoring. Confusion on these terms appears frequently.
⚠️ Trap 4 — RTO vs. MTD: RTO must be less than MTD. RTO + WRT ≤ MTD. If RTO equals MTD, there is zero tolerance for any data recovery delay (WRT = 0). This often trips up candidates.
⚠️ Trap 5 — Hot Site Speed: Hot sites can restore service in hours (or minutes for immediate failover). The exam sometimes implies they're "instantaneous" — in practice, some configuration, testing, and DNS changes are still needed, so "hours" not "seconds."
⚠️ Trap 6 — First Action on Termination: Always revoke access first — before HR meeting, before exit interview, before equipment retrieval (especially for hostile terminations).
