2. Real-World Organizational Relevance

Organizational Ownership

Asset Security spans multiple functions: Data Owners (business unit leaders) classify; Data Custodians (IT) implement protections; Legal/Compliance governs retention and GDPR; Privacy Officers oversee PII/PHI. The CISO orchestrates the security controls framework.

Implementation Reality

Real organizations implement asset security through: asset inventories and CMDB, classification labelling tools, DLP (network + endpoint + cloud), encryption at all three data states, data retention automation, and GDPR compliance platforms for ROPA and DPA management.

Failure Modes

  • Equifax (2017): 147M records breached — PII stored unencrypted, a failure of data-at-rest protection combined with poor classification and ownership.
  • Improper destruction: Hard drives sold with recoverable data — NIST 800-88 not followed.
  • Shadow IT: Staff storing classified data in personal cloud accounts bypasses all DLP and classification controls.

Business Impact

Poor asset security causes: regulatory fines (GDPR up to 4% global turnover), breach costs (IBM avg $4.45M in 2023), litigation, and reputational damage. Boards demand evidence via classification reports, DLP dashboards, and retention metrics.

6. Retention, Destruction & Remanence (Days 27 & 30)

NIST SP 800-88 Sanitization

LevelMethodResultUse When
ClearLogical overwriteUnrecoverable via standard tools; lab recovery possibleInternal reuse, same classification
PurgeDegaussing, crypto-erasure, firmware-level eraseUnrecoverable even with lab techniquesMedia leaving org control
DestroyShredding, incineration, pulverizingMedia physically unusableHighest sensitivity; end-of-life
⚠️ Degaussing ≠ SSD: Degaussing only works on magnetic media (HDDs, tapes). For SSDs/flash: use cryptographic erasure or physical destruction.

Data remanence = residual data remaining after deletion attempts. Standard deletion removes pointers, not data. Magnetic media retains ghost images. Flash wear-levelling preserves data in spare cells.

Litigation hold / legal hold overrides normal retention schedules — data must be preserved when litigation is reasonably anticipated, even if the retention period has expired.

10. High-Value Exam Traps

⚠️ Trap 1 — Owner vs. Custodian: Owner classifies; Custodian implements. Owner is always a business exec. "Who is accountable?" = Owner.
⚠️ Trap 2 — Pseudonymization ≠ Anonymization: Pseudonymized data is still personal data under GDPR. Only anonymized data is outside GDPR scope.
⚠️ Trap 3 — Degaussing SSDs: Degaussing only works on magnetic media. SSDs need crypto-erasure or physical destruction.
⚠️ Trap 4 — DPO Conflict: DPO cannot also be CISO, IT Director, CEO, Head of HR/Marketing — these roles determine processing purposes.
⚠️ Trap 5 — Right to Erasure: Not absolute — exceptions for legal claims, legal obligations, public health, freedom of expression, archiving.
⚠️ Trap 6 — 72-Hour Clock: Runs from when the controller learns of the breach, not the processor's discovery.

11. Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🧠 Gov Classification — "TS-S-C-SBU-U": Top Secret → Secret → Confidential → SBU → Unclassified. "Top Spies Carefully Secure Underground intel"
🧠 Data Roles: "Owner Decides, Custodian Guards, Steward Cleans, User Uses"
🧠 Data Lifecycle — "C-SUSAD": Create, Store, Use, Share, Archive, Destroy. "Can Students Usually Share Archived Documents?"
🧠 Data States — "RUT": at Rest, in Use, in Transit. "Data is always in some RUT"
🧠 NIST 800-88 — "CPD": Clear → Purge → Destroy (increasingly permanent)
🧠 DLP — "NEC": Network, Endpoint, Cloud/CASB
🧠 GDPR Roles: "Controller Commands; Processor Processes; Subject Owns; DPO Watches; SA Enforces"
🧠 Anon vs. Pseudo: "Anon = gone forever; Pseudo = pseudonym that can be reversed"
