1. Assessment of This 137-Day Training Course

This training is a custom-built 137-day HTML course covering all 8 CISSP domains. Each day is a standalone module of approximately 1,500–3,500 words (~45–75 min read), totalling the equivalent of a ~400–450 page study guide. Here is what it delivers well and where its gaps are:

You do not need another full-length theory textbook on top of this. What you need is a mindset supplement and far more practice questions.

4. Roadmap: 2-Month Theory Blitz + 2-Month Mastery

The Right Structure: Finish all 137 theory days in the first 2 months. Then spend the final 2 months on Destination CISSP, heavy cross-domain practice, mocks, and the exam. You walk into your first Boson mock with every domain already mapped in your head — exactly as the actual exam requires.
Why NOT spread theory over all 4 months: The CISSP exam draws from all 8 domains simultaneously. If you are finishing Domain 8 theory in Month 4 while trying to run cross-domain mocks, you will always be practicing against content you haven't fully seen yet. Finish the whole map first — then practice everything together.

Quality over Quantity: 3,300 questions with deep review of every wrong answer beats 5,000 questions clicked through mindlessly. The review step — asking "why did (ISC)² reject my answer?" — is where 80% of the learning happens.

How to Review Wrong Answers (Critical Process)

  1. Identify the domain and sub-topic of the question
  2. Find the corresponding theory day in this course and re-read the relevant section
  3. Identify whether you got it wrong due to: (a) knowledge gap, (b) mindset/framing, or (c) misreading the question stem
  4. For (a): make a flashcard and revisit in 3 days
  5. For (b): re-read the relevant chapter in Destination CISSP
  6. For (c): practice reading question stems by identifying the key action word (BEST, FIRST, MOST, LEAST)
CISSP Exam Answer Strategy: The exam almost always has two answers that are technically correct. The "right" answer is the one a security manager with budget authority and legal awareness would choose — not the most technically thorough option. When stuck: choose policy over technical, proactive over reactive, risk management over cure.

8. Exam Day Strategy

The CISSP CAT (Computerized Adaptive Testing) Format

CAT Format: CISSP uses Computer Adaptive Testing. You get 125–175 questions. The system adapts based on your answers — harder questions mean the system thinks you're competent. Finishing at 125 questions does NOT indicate pass or fail. The exam ends when the system is statistically confident you're above or below the passing threshold.
ParameterValue
Questions125–175 (CAT adaptive)
Time Limit4 hours
Question TypesMultiple choice + drag-and-drop + hotspot
Passing Score700 / 1000 (scaled)
LanguagesEnglish + others available
Pearson VUEBook at pearsonvue.com/isc2

On Exam Day

  • Read the stem twice — identify the key action word (BEST, FIRST, MOST important)
  • Eliminate obviously wrong answers first — you're usually choosing between 2 plausible answers
  • Think like a CISO, not a sysadmin — the exam rewards managerial, risk-aware thinking
  • Do not overthink — your first instinct after proper preparation is usually correct. Second-guessing is how prepared candidates fail
  • Budget time — 125 questions in 240 minutes = ~1.9 minutes per question. Do not spend more than 3 minutes on any single question; flag and return
  • Hydrate and eat beforehand — the test centre allows breaks but the clock does not stop
