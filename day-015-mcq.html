1A major flood forces a company to activate its continuity plans. The IT team restores servers and applications at the alternate site within 4 hours. Meanwhile, finance, HR, and customer service teams execute manual paper-based procedures to maintain critical business operations during IT recovery. Which of the following MOST accurately maps these activities to the correct plan types?
✅ Correct: B. BCP (Business Continuity Plan) addresses the continuation of critical BUSINESS FUNCTIONS during and after a disruption — including manual fallback procedures, alternate workflows, and temporary measures that keep the organisation operating when technology is unavailable. DRP (Disaster Recovery Plan) is a technical subset of BCP that focuses specifically on restoring IT systems, applications, and data to normal operating capability. The BCP is broader: it encompasses DRP plus people, processes, communications, facilities, and manual operations. Key CISSP distinction: DRP restores technology; BCP sustains business. DRP activates during the disruption; BCP governs the entire continuity lifecycle.
2A Business Impact Analysis establishes the following parameters for an order management system: RTO = 36 hours, WRT = 18 hours, MTD = 48 hours. Is the current recovery plan adequate, and why?
✅ Correct: B. Critical BCP timing formula: RTO + WRT must be ≤ MTD. Definitions: RTO (Recovery Time Objective) = maximum time to restore the system to a functional state. WRT (Work Recovery Time) = time required after system restoration to reprocess backlogs, reconcile data, and return to normal operations. MTD (Maximum Tolerable Downtime / MAO — Maximum Acceptable Outage) = maximum time the business can survive the disruption before suffering unacceptable consequences. RTO + WRT = 36 + 18 = 54 hours > MTD 48 hours. The plan is INADEQUATE — even if IT restores the system on time, the total time to full business normalisation (54h) exceeds the business tolerance (48h). The RTO must be reduced, or WRT must be reduced, or both.
3A financial trading firm determines its RPO for market position data at 4 hours. Which backup strategy MOST accurately satisfies this RPO requirement at minimum?
✅ Correct: D. RPO (Recovery Point Objective) defines the maximum amount of data loss that is acceptable, measured in time. An RPO of 4 hours means: in the worst case, recovery efforts should result in no more than 4 hours of lost transactions. To satisfy a 4-hour RPO: backups or replication must occur at intervals of AT MOST 4 hours. Option C is a valid approach (full + 4-hour log backups) but "at minimum" means ANY schedule with ≤ 4-hour backup intervals satisfies the RPO. Continuous/synchronous replication achieves RPO ≈ 0 (ideal). Daily backups (A) yield up to 24 hours of data loss — violates a 4-hour RPO. Weekly full + daily diff (B) yields up to 24 hours of loss — also violates.
4An organisation has never conducted BCP testing. The BCP team wants to choose an initial test type that provides value, builds familiarity with the plan, requires no live failover, and poses minimal operational risk. Which test type should they start with?
✅ Correct: C. BCP test types in increasing order of complexity and risk: (1) Checklist review — verify plan completeness against a checklist; (2) Structured walkthrough / Tabletop exercise — team verbally discusses their responses to a scenario; (3) Simulation — act-out a realistic scenario; (4) Parallel test — activate alternate site while production stays live; (5) Full interruption test — actual failover to alternate site, production taken offline. For a first-time test, a Tabletop exercise is appropriate: zero risk to production, builds team familiarity, identifies plan gaps and communication issues, requires only a meeting room and the plan document. Always build test maturity progressively — never start with a full interruption test.
5During a BCP test, the recovery team brings all alternate site systems online and validates data integrity and processing capability — while all production systems remain fully operational and serving live traffic. This test type is classified as:
✅ Correct: C. Parallel test: the alternate recovery site is brought fully online and validated (systems up, data loaded, processing confirmed), but production systems continue serving live traffic simultaneously — both environments run in parallel. Advantages: validates actual recovery capability; tests data synchronisation and alternate site functionality; no production risk. Full interruption test (A): production is actually taken offline and traffic shifted to alternate site — maximum realism but highest risk. Parallel (C) is the CISSP default "best of both worlds" test: it proves real recovery capability while protecting production availability. Organisations typically progress: Tabletop → Simulation → Parallel → Full interruption.
6A BCP steering committee is debating the appropriate role for senior management in the BCP program. A junior analyst suggests senior management's role is simply to fund BCP activities and receive summary reports. The security manager disagrees. Which of the following MOST accurately describes senior management's primary responsibilities in BCP?
✅ Correct: C. Senior management's BCP responsibilities (CISSP "manager mindset"): Programme sponsorship — authorise and champion the BCP programme; Risk appetite definition — establish acceptable levels of disruption and data loss (MTD, RTO, RPO targets); Policy approval — formally approve BCP policy and scope; Resource allocation — commit budget, personnel, and technology; Residual risk acceptance — formally accept risks that the BCP cannot fully mitigate; Accountability — bear ultimate legal and fiduciary responsibility for organisational resilience. Senior management CANNOT delegate this accountability. The BCP coordinator manages execution; senior management owns governance and accountability. This is the most important CISSP principle: management accountability cannot be delegated away.
7During a BIA, analysts discover that a retail company's primary revenue-generating system (online store) generates 40% of annual revenue per day during peak season. Analysis shows that at Day 7 of continuous outage, the company would be unable to meet payroll, service debt, or fulfil critical supplier contracts, resulting in potential insolvency. What BCP/BIA term MOST precisely describes this Day 7 threshold?
✅ Correct: C. Maximum Tolerable Downtime (MTD) — also called Maximum Acceptable Outage (MAO) — is the absolute maximum time an organisation can survive a system or process disruption before suffering unacceptable, potentially irreversible harm. The Day 7 threshold (insolvency risk) is the textbook definition of MTD: beyond this point, the organisation cannot recover to a viable state. MTD is determined by the BIA and drives BCP design: RTO + WRT must be < MTD. MTD is the BUSINESS CONSTRAINT that makes BCP planning a critical necessity, not just a best practice. It translates business impact into technical recovery requirements by establishing the hard deadline that BCP objectives must satisfy.
8A financial exchange sets an RPO of zero (0) for its trade execution audit log. What data replication strategy is required to achieve RPO = 0?
✅ Correct: B. RPO = 0 means ZERO data loss is acceptable — no transactions can be lost under any failure condition. This requires synchronous replication: every write is simultaneously confirmed on both primary and secondary storage before the application receives confirmation. If primary fails at any point, every committed transaction exists on the secondary. Asynchronous replication (C) introduces a replication lag (seconds to minutes) — any transactions in the replication buffer at time of failure are lost. Hourly backups (A) yield up to 60 minutes of data loss. RAID mirroring (D) protects against local disk failure only — not site-level failures. Synchronous replication has cost/performance implications (write latency increases) making it reserved for the most critical RPO=0 workloads.
9An organisation's BCM (Business Continuity Management) program requires certification against a recognised international standard for business continuity management systems. Which standard provides a certifiable BCM framework with internationally recognised organisational certification?
✅ Correct: B. ISO 22301 (Security and Resilience — Business Continuity Management Systems — Requirements) is the internationally recognised, certifiable standard for BCM. Organisations can obtain third-party ISO 22301 certification demonstrating their BCMS meets international requirements. Key sections: Context, Leadership, Planning, Support, Operations, Performance evaluation, Improvement — following the standard ISO PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) structure. ISO 27001 (C) covers information security broadly and includes Business Continuity as a control domain (Annex A 17), but it is not a dedicated BCM certification standard. NIST 800-34 (A) is US government guidance, not a certifiable standard. COBIT (D) is a governance framework, not a certifiable BCM standard.
10Following a major ransomware attack, an organisation successfully activates its BCP and restores all systems within RTO targets. Once the crisis is resolved and normal operations are fully restored, what is the MOST important immediate next step for the BCP program?
✅ Correct: B. Post-incident after-action review (AAR) / lessons-learned is a mandatory BCP lifecycle activity. Every real-world BCP activation provides irreplaceable evidence about plan effectiveness that no exercise can replicate. The AAR must: document what occurred chronologically; identify decisions that worked and decisions that failed; quantify actual RTO/RPO vs. targets; identify plan gaps, outdated contact information, missing procedures; document improvements required. All findings must be fed back into the BCP, DRP, and related plans with formal plan updates, re-training, and schedule for re-testing improved procedures. BCP is a living document — without post-incident learning, the plan remains static while the threat landscape and organisation evolve. This is the "continuous improvement" principle central to ISO 22301 and CISSP BCM doctrine.
