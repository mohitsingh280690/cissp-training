1An organisation publishes a document specifying the exact minimum configuration settings (e.g., TLS 1.2 minimum, AES-256, 15-minute screen lock) that must be applied to all Windows 11 workstations. This document is BEST classified as which type of policy instrument?
✅ Correct: D. A Security Baseline defines the minimum mandatory security configuration for a specific technology type (e.g., Windows workstations, web servers, network switches). It is derived from standards and is mandatory — all systems of that type must meet or exceed the baseline. CIS Benchmarks and DISA STIGs are industry-standard baseline examples. A Security Standard dictates what must be done; a Baseline specifies the specific technical settings that satisfy that standard for a given system class. Guidelines (B) are recommendations, not mandates.
2A security team publishes a document stating: "When connecting to cloud services from untrusted networks, employees are encouraged to consider using a VPN client configured with split tunneling disabled." This document carries no disciplinary consequence if ignored. This is BEST classified as:
✅ Correct: D. A Security Guideline is advisory — it provides recommended best-practice guidance but is not mandatory and carries no enforcement mechanism. Keywords: "encouraged," "consider," "should." Guidelines give practitioners flexibility when situational context varies. This is distinct from Standards (mandatory "shalls") and Procedures (mandatory step-by-step). The policy hierarchy from high to specific: Policy → Standard → Procedure → Baseline → Guideline.
3NIST released an update to the Cybersecurity Framework (CSF 2.0) in 2024. What is the most significant structural change compared to CSF 1.1?
✅ Correct: B. NIST CSF 2.0 (February 2024) added the GOVERN function as its most significant structural addition. GOVERN emphasises: organisational context, risk management strategy, roles and responsibilities, policy, and supply chain risk management. The original five functions (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover) remain. GOVERN recognises that governance activities enable and underpin all other functions. CSF remains voluntary for private sector; SP 800-53 remains the mandatory control catalog for federal agencies.
4An organisation's Information Security Policy states: "All data must be protected against unauthorised access in accordance with its classification level." A security manager argues the policy should also specify that AES-256 encryption must be used. Under sound policy architecture principles, is the manager correct?
✅ Correct: B. Security policies should be high-level, principle-based, and technology-agnostic. Embedding specific technical requirements (like AES-256) in policy means the entire policy must be revised every time cryptographic standards evolve. Technical specifics belong in Security Standards (mandatory) and Baselines (system-specific mins). This creates a stable, durable policy layer with a more agile technical layer beneath it. The policy sets the "what and why"; standards and procedures set the "how."
5An organisation wants to pursue ISO 27001 certification to demonstrate its information security management system to customers and regulators. A colleague suggests implementing ISO 27002 controls instead for the same certification benefit. What is the key distinction?
✅ Correct: B. ISO 27001 specifies the requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining and continually improving an ISMS — it uses "shall" language and is auditable by accredited certification bodies. ISO 27002 provides guidance on implementing the controls listed in Annex A of ISO 27001 — it uses "should" language and is a code of practice, not a certifiable standard. Organisations get certified to ISO 27001; they use ISO 27002 as implementation guidance. Both were updated in 2022.
6During an audit, an organisation's security program is assessed as having: documented policies approved by management, regular security awareness training, and procedures that are consistently followed. However, the processes are not yet formally optimised or measured. Under the SSE-CMM maturity model, this MOST closely represents which maturity tier?
✅ Correct: C. SSE-CMM (Systems Security Engineering Capability Maturity Model) Level 3 "Well Defined" means: standard processes are documented, approved by management, and consistently used across the organisation. There is an organisational standard process set. Level 2 means individual projects are planned/tracked. Level 4 adds quantitative measurement. Level 5 adds continuous improvement based on data. The scenario — documented policies, approved procedures, consistent execution — maps to Level 3. The absence of quantitative measurement rules out Levels 4–5.
7An organisation distributes its Acceptable Use Policy to all employees at onboarding and requires a signed acknowledgment. One year later, an employee is disciplined for violating the AUP but claims: "I never knew personal device use was prohibited on the corporate network." What is the PRIMARY legal and governance value of the signed acknowledgment?
✅ Correct: B. Signed policy acknowledgments are a foundational governance control — they provide documented proof that the employee received, read, and agreed to the policies. This is essential for: (1) supporting HR disciplinary actions without an "ignorance" defence; (2) establishing a legal basis for termination for cause; (3) demonstrating due diligence in security governance audits. Policies are only enforceable if employees are demonstrably notified. Annual re-signing is recommended as policies change and to ensure continued acknowledgment.
8A CISO is tasked with aligning the organisation's security governance program to a framework that helps demonstrate how security investments directly support and enable business objectives, and provides IT governance guidance to the board. Which framework is MOST appropriate?
✅ Correct: C. COBIT (published by ISACA) is specifically designed to align IT governance with business objectives. It provides: governance and management objectives, performance management, and a framework for presenting IT strategy to the board. NIST SP 800-53 (A) is a controls catalog focused on federal compliance. ISO 27001 (B) focuses on ISMS certification. NIST CSF (D) focuses on cybersecurity risk management. COBIT is the go-to framework when the question is about business-IT alignment, board reporting, or demonstrating IT's value to the organisation.
9An organisation's written standard states: "All user account passwords must be at least 12 characters, include uppercase, lowercase, number, and special character, and be changed every 90 days." This document is BEST described as:
✅ Correct: C. A Security Standard contains specific, mandatory, measurable requirements that tell implementers exactly what must be achieved (the "what"). It's more specific than a Policy but does not prescribe step-by-step implementation steps (that's a Procedure). Password standards are a classic example: exact length, complexity rules, change frequency — all measurable and mandatory. A Baseline (B) goes further to specify system-specific technical configurations (e.g., Group Policy Object settings to enforce these requirements on Windows). The difference: Standards = what is required; Baselines = specific system settings to achieve it.
10An organisation has implemented an ISO 27001-aligned ISMS and conducts monthly internal audits and quarterly management reviews to verify control effectiveness. This continual improvement activity maps to which phase of the ISO PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycle?
✅ Correct: C. The "Check" phase of the PDCA cycle encompasses: internal audits, management reviews, performance measurement against KPIs and KRIs, monitoring of control effectiveness, and compliance checks. The other phases: Plan = establish scope, risk assessment, risk treatment, controls selection; Do = implement selected controls, training, procedures; Act = take corrective/preventive actions, update policies based on lessons learned. Internal audits + management reviews are the defining activities of the Check phase in an ISO 27001-aligned ISMS.
