1. Domain 7 – Security Operations (13%)

Incident Response Phases

PhaseKey ActivitiesExam Focus
1. PreparationIR plan, team, tools, trainingMost important phase; done BEFORE incidents
2. Detection & AnalysisIDS/SIEM alerts, log analysis, triageDetermine if event is actually an incident
3. ContainmentShort-term + long-term containmentFIRST priority after detection; prevent spread
4. EradicationRemove cause, patch, rebuildEliminate root cause completely
5. RecoveryRestore systems, verify, monitorReturn to normal operations; validate security
6. Lessons LearnedPost-incident review, update plansMost often skipped; improves future response
After detection, the FIRST action is always CONTAINMENT — not eradication, not investigation. Contain the damage first, then investigate. Also: preserve evidence BEFORE reimaging systems.

Business Continuity Metrics

MetricDefinitionRelationship
MTDMaximum Tolerable DowntimeOuter boundary; exceeding = business failure
RTORecovery Time ObjectiveTarget time to restore service; must be < MTD
RPORecovery Point ObjectiveMax acceptable data loss (time); drives backup frequency
WRTWork Recovery TimeTime to verify restored systems; RTO + WRT ≤ MTD
Critical formula: RTO + WRT ≤ MTD. If RTO = 4 hours and WRT = 2 hours, then MTD must be at least 6 hours. RPO determines backup frequency: RPO of 1 hour = backup every hour.

4. Incident Response & SDLC Master Reference

IR Priorities (In Order)

  1. Life safety — always #1 priority in ANY scenario
  2. Containment — stop the bleeding
  3. Evidence preservation — before eradication
  4. Eradication — remove root cause
  5. Recovery — restore operations
  6. Notification — legal/regulatory requirements
CISSP Priority Rule: Safety of human life ALWAYS comes first. If a question has "protect people" as an option, that's the answer — before systems, data, or business operations.
