2. Real-World Organizational Relevance

Organizations without tested DR plans face existential risk. After the 2011 Tohoku earthquake and tsunami, organizations with offshore DR sites recovered in hours; those without lost months of operations. Cloud adoption has changed DR economics — cloud-based DR is more accessible but introduces new dependencies (cloud provider outages, region-specific disasters, shared responsibility model).

3. DRP vs BCP — The Key Distinction

AspectBCP (Business Continuity Planning)DRP (Disaster Recovery Planning)
ScopeEntire organization — people, processes, technology, facilitiesIT systems and infrastructure recovery specifically
FocusMaintaining critical business FUNCTIONS during and after disruptionRestoring IT SYSTEMS and DATA after a disaster
Key deliverableBusiness Continuity Plan — how the business keeps runningDisaster Recovery Plan — how IT recovers
Driven byBusiness Impact Analysis (BIA)BCP requirements (BIA determines what IT must recover and how fast)
OwnerBusiness leadership / executive managementIT / CISO / infrastructure team
TimelineDuring disruption — maintain operationsAfter disruption — restore normalcy
Exam Relationship: DRP is a SUBSET of BCP. BCP is the umbrella; DRP handles the technical IT recovery component. The BIA (from BCP) determines WHICH systems must be recovered and HOW FAST (RTO/RPO) — DRP then builds the plan to meet those requirements.

6. Recovery Objectives — RTO, RPO, MTD, WRT

MetricFull NameDefinitionExample
MTDMaximum Tolerable DowntimeThe absolute MAXIMUM time a business function can be unavailable before the organization suffers unacceptable damage (financial, legal, reputational)Email system: MTD = 48 hours; Payment processing: MTD = 4 hours
RTORecovery Time ObjectiveThe TARGET time to restore a system/function after a disruption. Must be LESS than MTDEmail: RTO = 24 hours; Payment: RTO = 1 hour
WRTWork Recovery TimeTime needed to verify systems work correctly, restore data integrity, and resume normal operations AFTER systems are technically restoredAfter email server is running (RTO met), WRT = 4 hours to verify all mailboxes, test send/receive, restore pending queues
RPORecovery Point ObjectiveThe MAXIMUM acceptable amount of DATA LOSS measured in time. How far back can we go?RPO = 1 hour means we can afford to lose up to 1 hour of data (backups must be at least hourly)
RECOVERY TIMELINE ← RPO ──────────── Disaster ──────── RTO ──── WRT ──── MTD → │ Last good backup Event System Verified Maximum (data loss point) occurs restored & normal tolerable (tech) (business) downtime KEY RELATIONSHIP: RTO + WRT ≤ MTD
Critical Relationship: RTO + WRT must be ≤ MTD. The system recovery time (RTO) plus the time to verify and resume normal operations (WRT) cannot exceed the maximum tolerable downtime (MTD). If RTO + WRT > MTD, the recovery strategy is inadequate and must be improved.
RPO Drives Backup Frequency: RPO = 4 hours means backups must occur at least every 4 hours. RPO = 0 (zero data loss) requires synchronous replication — the most expensive option. RPO is about DATA; RTO is about TIME to restore SYSTEMS.

7. DR Team Roles & Responsibilities

RoleResponsibilities
DR Coordinator / ManagerOverall DR plan ownership; activation authority; coordinates all recovery activities; keeps management informed
IT Recovery TeamExecute technical recovery procedures; restore servers, databases, applications, networks
Damage Assessment TeamEvaluate impact of disaster on primary site; determine when primary site can be reoccupied
Communications TeamInternal and external communications; media relations; customer notifications; regulatory notifications
Logistics / Operations TeamTransportation; alternate work locations; equipment procurement; vendor coordination
Salvage TeamRecover and preserve assets from the damaged primary site; coordinate with insurance
⚠️ Named Alternates Required: Every role must have a designated alternate. During an actual disaster, primary personnel may be unavailable (injured, unreachable, personally affected). The plan must function with alternate personnel.

8. Communication Plans During Disaster

Communication Requirements

  • Call tree / notification cascade: Who calls whom; automated notification systems preferred (mass notification tools)
  • Out-of-band communication: Primary phone/email systems may be down — use personal cell phones, satellite phones, offline meeting points
  • Stakeholder-specific messaging: Different messages for employees, customers, regulators, media, investors
  • Single spokesperson: All external communications through designated spokesperson to ensure consistent messaging
  • Regular status updates: Scheduled intervals for status reporting (even if "no change")

9. Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🧠 Recovery Metrics — "RPO = data PAST, RTO = time to RESTORE"
RPO looks BACKWARD — how much data can we LOSE?
RTO looks FORWARD — how fast must we RECOVER?
WRT = "after RTO, verify it WORKS"
MTD = "the absolute MAX, cannot exceed"
🧠 The Critical Formula
RTO + WRT ≤ MTD
"Recovery Time + Work Recovery must fit inside Maximum Tolerable Downtime"
🧠 BCP vs DRP
BCP = "Keep the Business running" (umbrella — people, process, technology)
DRP = "Recover the Data center" (subset — IT infrastructure specifically)
"BCP is the parent; DRP is the child"
🧠 RPO Drives Backups
"RPO = 1 hour → backup every hour. RPO = 0 → synchronous replication (real-time). The smaller the RPO, the bigger the cost."
