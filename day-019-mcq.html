MCQ Practice – Domain 1: Security and Risk Management

Question 1: An organisation's developers are consistently introducing SQL injection vulnerabilities into production code. What is the BEST intervention to address this root cause?

✅ Correct: B. The root cause is a skill gap — developers lack secure coding knowledge. Role-based training specifically addressing OWASP Top 10, input validation, and parameterised queries directly addresses this. A WAF (option A) is a compensating technical control, not root cause remediation. Organisation-wide awareness (C) is too shallow for a role-specific technical skill gap. Certification (D) is education-tier, not the targeted skill training needed.

Question 2: Which of the following BEST demonstrates that a security awareness programme is achieving its intended outcome?

✅ Correct: B. A reduction in phishing click rate is a direct behavioural outcome measure — it shows the programme changed real-world behaviour. Completion rate (A) measures activity (did employees watch?), not whether behaviour changed. Budget increases (C) are input measures. Posting posters (D) is an activity, not a measured outcome.

Question 3: An attacker calls the help desk, impersonates a senior executive, and persuades a technician to reset the executive's password without verifying identity. This attack is BEST classified as:

✅ Correct: C. Vishing (voice phishing) is social engineering conducted over telephone calls. The attacker uses impersonation (a psychological authority trigger) to manipulate the help desk technician. This is a classic vishing attack targeting the help desk as the weakest link in credential management. Phishing (A) is email-based; baiting (B) involves physical media; tailgating (D) is physical access.

Question 4: According to the three-tier awareness/training/education model, which tier is MOST appropriate for all newly hired employees regardless of role?

✅ Correct: C. Security awareness is the broad, shallow foundation that applies to ALL staff. It focuses on recognition — recognising phishing, lock screen compliance, clean desk policy, reporting incidents. Training is role-specific, and education is for security professionals. All new employees, regardless of role, go through awareness first.

Question 5: Which NIST publication provides comprehensive guidance on building an information security awareness and training programme?

✅ Correct: D. NIST SP 800-50 is specifically titled "Building an Information Technology Security Awareness and Training Program." SP 800-53 covers security controls; SP 800-61 covers incident response; SP 800-88 covers media sanitisation. Know these reference-to-publication mappings for the exam.

Question 6: A finance team member receives an email appearing to come from the CFO requesting an urgent wire transfer to a new vendor. The email uses the CFO's exact name and writing style. This is BEST described as:

✅ Correct: C. This is a Business Email Compromise (BEC) attack — a highly targeted spear phishing attack impersonating an executive to authorise fraudulent financial transactions. The finance team member is the target, not a C-suite executive (whaling targets the executive themselves). The email impersonation with personalised details is spear phishing at its most effective. BEC is one of the most financially damaging attack vectors annually.

Question 7: An employee plugs in a USB drive found in the office car park, hoping it might contain something interesting. The drive installs malware. What social engineering technique does this MOST represent?

✅ Correct: C. Baiting exploits curiosity or greed by leaving physical media (USB drives, CDs, SD cards) in locations where targets will find and use them. The "car park USB drop" is the canonical baiting scenario. This technique is well-documented in physical penetration tests. Controls include USB port lockdown policies, endpoint protection for USB media, and awareness training specifically addressing found media.

Question 8: After delivering security awareness training, management wants to assess whether the programme is worth continuing. Which of the following BEST demonstrates return on investment (ROI) of the programme?

✅ Correct: C. ROI is best demonstrated through outcome metrics — specifically a measurable reduction in incidents causally linked to the behaviour the training targeted. This directly translates to cost avoidance (fewer breaches = lower remediation costs, fewer fines, reduced liability). Production volume (A), satisfaction (B), and interest rates (D) are all activity or sentiment measures that don't demonstrate business impact.

Question 9: An organisation's security team wants to ensure that training content is updated to address emerging threats. Which principle does this MOST align with?

✅ Correct: C. Continuous improvement in security awareness programmes ensures content evolves with the threat landscape. Annual reviews are the minimum; threat-triggered updates (e.g., when a new phishing campaign is observed targeting the sector) should happen as needed. This is the "maintain" phase in NIST SP 800-50 lifecycle. Due diligence (B) relates to performing adequate research before decisions, which is secondary here.

Question 10: Which of the following represents the HIGHEST tier in the NIST/ISC² awareness-training-education hierarchy?

✅ Correct: D. Education is the highest tier — it builds deep, theoretical, and strategic understanding and is aimed at security professionals and leadership. A graduate degree in information security is the clearest example of education-tier activity. Phishing simulation (A) and tip newsletters (C) are awareness-tier; role-based admin training (B) is training-tier.
