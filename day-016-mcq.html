Domain 1 – Security and Risk Management

1A BIA determines that an e-commerce order processing system has: RTO = 6 hours, WRT = 3 hours, MTD = 8 hours. Which statement BEST characterises the current recovery plan?
✅ Correct: B. The constraint is RTO + WRT ≤ MTD. Here 6 + 3 = 9 > 8 — the plan is inadequate. Recovery is not complete when the system comes back up (RTO); it is complete when the business returns to normal operations (RTO + WRT). MTD is the absolute business limit, not a soft target. Option A makes the common error of comparing only RTO against MTD and ignoring WRT.
2A financial trading platform sets an RPO of zero. Which data protection strategy is the ONLY way to technically achieve RPO = 0?
✅ Correct: C. RPO = 0 means zero data loss under any failure condition. Only synchronous replication achieves this — each write is committed to both primary and secondary simultaneously before the application receives confirmation. Asynchronous replication (B) has a replication lag (seconds to minutes), so transactions in the buffer at failure time are lost. Any backup-based approach (A, D) introduces a window of data loss equal to the time since the last backup.
3During a BIA, the team is deciding which department should define the MTD for the customer relationship management (CRM) system. Who should have FINAL authority over this determination?
✅ Correct: C. MTD is a BUSINESS requirement, not an IT requirement. The business process owner understands the financial, operational, and contractual consequences of CRM downtime. IT provides capability inputs (what is technically achievable and at what cost), and the BCP coordinator facilitates, but the business owner has final authority over setting the MTD because they bear the business consequences. The CISO's role is advisory, not decision-making authority on business metrics.
4A server has an MTBF of 2,000 hours and an MTTR of 40 hours. What is its availability percentage?
✅ Correct: C. Availability = MTBF ÷ (MTBF + MTTR) = 2000 ÷ (2000 + 40) = 2000 ÷ 2040 = 0.9804 = 98.04%. This is slightly above 98% — not acceptable for a critical system. To reach 99.99%, either increase MTBF dramatically (more reliable hardware) or decrease MTTR (faster repair — through redundancy and automation), or both.
5During a disaster recovery scenario, a security manager must prioritise which systems to restore first. The BIA shows: System A has MTD 4 hours; System B has MTD 24 hours; System C is a shared authentication service that Systems A and B both depend on, with MTD 8 hours. In what order should recovery proceed?
✅ Correct: B. System C (authentication) must be restored first because A and B both depend on it — restoring A before C is pointless since A cannot function without authentication. Even though A has the shortest MTD (4 hours), the upstream dependency means C must come first. This is the "dependency ordering" principle: restore foundational shared services before dependent applications. After C, restore A (shortest remaining MTD), then B.
6A company's payroll system has RPO = 24 hours. A ransomware attack destroys all production data at 10:00 AM on a Thursday. The last successful backup was taken at 11:00 PM Wednesday. What data loss scenario does this represent?
✅ Correct: B. The backup at 11:00 PM Wednesday is 11 hours before the 10:00 AM Thursday attack. Since RPO = 24 hours, data loss up to 24 hours is acceptable. 11 hours of data loss is within the 24-hour RPO — the RPO is technically met. The organisation will need to manually re-enter or reconstruct the 11 hours of transactions, but this is within the accepted business tolerance. The challenge is operational (re-entry effort) but not a BIA compliance failure.
7What is the key difference between the BIA and a traditional risk assessment in BCP context?
✅ Correct: B. The BIA focuses on IMPACT — what does the loss of this function cost the business over time, regardless of what caused the disruption. The risk assessment focuses on PROBABILITY — which threats are likely to occur and cause disruption. Both are needed: the BIA drives recovery requirements (RTO, RPO, MTD); the risk assessment drives prevention investment. They are complementary but distinct analyses performed as part of the broader BCP lifecycle.
8A hospital BIA reveals that the patient monitoring system has MTD = 0 (continuous availability required). However, the electronic health record (EHR) system shows MTD = 4 hours. A power failure simultaneously knocks out both. What should the incident response team prioritise FIRST?
✅ Correct: B. Life safety always supersedes all other recovery priorities. Patient monitoring = direct life-safety function. The first action is always protecting people — activating emergency procedures, manual monitoring protocols, notifying clinical staff. Then restore the life-safety system (monitoring) immediately. EHR restoration follows. This is a fundamental CISSP principle: no matter what the BIA priority order shows, personnel safety takes precedence over any system recovery timeline.
9MTBF for a critical system is 500 hours. What does this metric MOST accurately indicate about the system?
✅ Correct: B. MTBF is a statistical average — the expected time the system operates between failures. MTBF = 500 hours means roughly one failure every 500 hours (~21 days), which is quite frequent for a critical system. This is distinct from MTTR (repair time — C). MTBF cannot predict exactly when the next failure will occur (D is wrong). For critical systems, low MTBF drives the business decision to implement redundancy (RAID, clustering, failover) to achieve acceptable availability despite component failure rates.
10During BIA interviews, the VP of Operations states that the logistics system "should be back within a day." The IT team believes they can restore it in 36 hours under the current architecture. Which scenario MOST accurately describes the implication of these statements?
✅ Correct: B. This is a classic BIA finding: business expectations and IT capability are misaligned. The VP's "within a day" is an informal MTD indicator — if formalised as MTD = 24 hours, then IT's 36-hour recovery capability violates it. The BIA process must: (1) formally document the business requirement as MTD; (2) document IT's current capability as candidate RTO; (3) identify the gap; (4) drive a decision — invest in faster recovery capability, or present the gap to management for formal risk acceptance. Neither party alone resolves this — it requires a business decision.
