Domain 1 – Security and Risk Management

1A software developer employed by a technology company writes a new algorithm during work hours using company resources, solving a business problem assigned by her manager. Who holds the copyright for the resulting software?
✅ Correct: C. Under US copyright law (17 U.S.C. § 101), a "work made for hire" created by an employee within the scope of employment is owned by the employer from the moment of creation — no assignment agreement is required. This is a critical CISSP concept: employers own IP created by employees as part of their duties. Consultants and contractors are different — independent contractor work requires a written agreement to qualify as work-for-hire.
2A developer incorporates 20 lines of code from an open-source library licensed under GPL v3 into a proprietary commercial product and ships the binary without releasing any source code. What is the MOST significant legal risk?
✅ Correct: B. GPL v3 is a "copyleft" license — if you distribute software that incorporates GPL-licensed code, you must distribute the entire work under GPL v3 and make source code available. Shipping a proprietary binary without source code violates the copyleft terms. This is a common software due diligence issue. GPL v3 does NOT prohibit commercial use — only requires that derivatives remain open-source. CISSP candidates must understand the difference between permissive licenses (MIT, BSD) and copyleft licenses (GPL).
3An organization invents a novel network intrusion detection process that has never been done before and is not obvious to experts in the field. Which type of legal protection provides the STRONGEST exclusive rights for up to 20 years?
✅ Correct: B. A utility patent (20 years from filing date) protects novel, non-obvious, useful processes, machines, manufactures, or compositions of matter. For an intrusion detection process, a method patent provides the strongest exclusive rights — the holder can prevent anyone from using the process regardless of whether they independently discovered it. Trade secrets (C) provide no protection if the process is independently discovered or reverse-engineered. Copyright (A) protects the specific code expression, not the underlying process/algorithm itself.
4A company fails to implement any access controls, NDAs, or confidentiality policies around its proprietary machine learning model. A competitor independently develops an identical model without any contact with the first company. The first company sues for trade secret misappropriation. What is the most likely outcome?
✅ Correct: B. Under both the Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA) and the Uniform Trade Secrets Act (UTSA), a trade secret exists only if the owner takes "reasonable measures" to keep the information secret. No NDAs, no access controls, no confidentiality policies = no reasonable measures = likely no trade secret protection. Additionally, independent development (without misappropriation) is a complete defense to trade secret claims — unlike patent law, independent discovery is permitted. Option D is wrong because trade secrets specifically avoid registration.
5A social media platform hosts millions of user-uploaded videos, some of which contain copyrighted music. The platform has a working DMCA notice-and-takedown process and promptly removes content when notified. Under the DMCA safe harbor (17 U.S.C. § 512), which statement BEST describes the platform's liability?
✅ Correct: B. The DMCA safe harbor (§512) protects Online Service Providers (OSPs) from secondary copyright liability for user-generated content, provided they: designate a DMCA agent, have a policy to terminate repeat infringers, do not have knowledge of infringement, do not financially benefit directly from infringing activity (while having the right and ability to control it), and expeditiously remove content upon proper notice. Option D describes proactive monitoring — the safe harbor intentionally does NOT require proactive monitoring, which would be an enormous legal burden.
6A startup names its cloud storage product "Googol Drive" with a stylised logo similar to a well-known search engine company's branding. Under the Lanham Act, the search engine company's trademark infringement claim would primarily require demonstrating:
✅ Correct: B. Trademark infringement under the Lanham Act turns on the "likelihood of confusion" standard — would ordinary consumers likely be confused about the source, sponsorship, or affiliation of the goods/services? Courts apply multi-factor tests (Sleekcraft factors) including similarity of marks, similarity of goods/services, channels of trade, and consumer sophistication. Intent to copy (C) is relevant but not required — you can infringe unintentionally. Actual lost revenue (D) is not required to prove likelihood of confusion.
7A security researcher circumvents the copy-protection system on a legitimately purchased video game to extract short clips for use in an academic presentation critiquing the game's narrative. Under the DMCA, which statement MOST accurately describes the legal situation?
✅ Correct: B. DMCA § 1201 creates a separate anti-circumvention right that operates independently from copyright's fair use doctrine. Courts have held that fair use does not automatically justify circumventing technological protection measures. The US Copyright Office does create limited exemptions every 3 years (some for security research), but these are narrow and specific — there is no blanket academic exemption. This is a classic CISSP trap: the assumption that fair use = permission to circumvent DRM.
8During a software asset management audit, a company discovers it has deployed 750 copies of a database application across its enterprise. However, its licence agreement only covers 400 seats. What is the MOST accurate description of the company's legal exposure?
✅ Correct: B. Software is protected by copyright. Each unlicensed copy is a separate act of copying protected expression, constituting copyright infringement beyond contractual breach. Vendors may pursue claims under the Copyright Act, which provides statutory damages of $750–$30,000 per work infringed (up to $150,000 for wilful infringement). SAM (Software Asset Management) programs exist precisely to prevent this exposure. Option D understates the risk — copyright infringement and contractual breach can coexist.
9An engineer leaves Company A and deliberately takes a confidential technical specification document marked "Trade Secret" to Company B (a direct competitor), which then uses it to accelerate product development. Under the Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA), which parties may face liability?
✅ Correct: C. Under the DTSA (18 U.S.C. § 1839), both the misappropriating individual AND the entity that knowingly acquires and uses misappropriated trade secrets can be held liable. Civil remedies include injunctive relief, damages, and exemplary damages (2×). Criminal penalties can apply to wilful, intentional theft (18 U.S.C. § 1832). Company B "knowing" use of stolen trade secrets makes it a direct participant in the misappropriation. The DTSA was specifically designed to address the scenario of employees stealing IP for competitors.
10A novelist finishes writing a thriller novel and saves it to her computer — she has told no one about it and has not published or registered it anywhere. Which statement about her intellectual property rights is MOST accurate?
✅ Correct: D. Under the Copyright Act of 1976 (17 U.S.C. § 102), copyright protection arises automatically at the moment an original work is fixed in a tangible medium of expression. No registration, publication, or © notice is required for protection. However, registration IS required before filing a lawsuit in the US and enables statutory damages. The Berne Convention (adopted 1989 by the US) eliminated the previous requirement for copyright notice. Key CISSP point: copyright is automatic, patents require application and examination.
