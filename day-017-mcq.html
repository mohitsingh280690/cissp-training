Day 17: BCP Strategy & Plan Development – Practice Quiz

Domain 1 – Security and Risk Management
📝 10 Questions🎯 CISSP Level✅ Pass: 7/10
Instructions: Select the BEST answer for each question. Click Submit Answers to reveal explanations and your score. Pass threshold: 7/10 (70%).
1A trading platform requires an RTO of 90 minutes. Which recovery site type is the MINIMUM requirement to meet this objective?
✅ Correct: C. A 90-minute RTO requires a hot site — a fully equipped, powered, and loaded facility that can receive live traffic within minutes to hours of activation. Cold sites take days to weeks; warm sites take hours to days. A reciprocal agreement is unreliable (the partner facility may be unavailable) and activation time is uncontrolled. For any RTO under 4 hours, a hot site (or equivalent cloud failover) is required.
2An organisation's payroll system BIA sets MTD = 5 days. Management asks the BCP architect to select the most cost-effective recovery site strategy. Which choice is MOST appropriate?
✅ Correct: B. With MTD = 5 days, a cold site is the most cost-effective choice. Cold sites need days to weeks to activate — well within the 5-day tolerance. A hot site would be three to five times more expensive without adding necessary value. The correct BCP principle is: select the cheapest strategy that satisfies the MTD/RTO requirement — not the most capable one. Hot sites are expensive and justified only for very short MTDs. Reciprocal agreements (D) are unreliable and unsuitable for systems requiring guaranteed recovery timelines.
3A fire suppression system activates in the primary data centre, destroying all servers. The BCP team activates the DRP and begins restoring systems at the warm site. According to BCP best practice, what should be the VERY FIRST action taken when the alarm is triggered?
✅ Correct: C. Personnel safety is ALWAYS the first priority in any disaster — no system recovery action precedes ensuring human safety. This is a foundational CISSP principle that appears in multiple exam questions. Only after all personnel are safe and accounted for should the BCP/DRP activation sequence begin. The exam will consistently present option A as tempting — resist it. Systems can be restored; people cannot be replaced.
4A company and a peer organisation sign a reciprocal agreement allowing each to use the other's data centre in the event of a disaster. A regional earthquake renders both organisations' facilities unusable simultaneously. What does this scenario MOST expose about reciprocal agreements?
✅ Correct: C. Reciprocal agreements have three fundamental weaknesses: (1) geographic correlation risk — a regional disaster affects both parties; (2) capacity risk — the partner building may not have enough physical or computing capacity to absorb a full failover; (3) testing risk — agreements are rarely operationally tested so problems are discovered during the actual disaster. The CISSP exam consistently presents reciprocal agreements as the "low cost but unreliable" distractor — not appropriate for critical systems requiring guaranteed availability.
5The BCP document for a financial services firm is stored exclusively on the company's primary file server. The primary file server is destroyed in a flood. Which vital records management failure does this represent?
✅ Correct: C. The BCP is itself a vital record — one of the most critical ones. If the plan is destroyed in the same disaster it was designed to address, it cannot be executed. Vital records management requires: identifying all vital records (including the BCP, DRP, runbooks, system configs), classifying them by criticality, and storing copies offsite / in physically separate locations accessible without access to the primary site. Many organisations maintain printed copies at designated personnel's homes or in fire-proof offsite storage.
6During a BCP parallel test, the team successfully brings all alternate site systems online and validates data integrity while production systems continue operating normally. Test results show that the authentication server at the alternate site takes 4 hours to fully initialise — but the RTO for all dependent systems is 3 hours. What is the correct next step?
✅ Correct: B. Parallel test findings that reveal gaps between tested capability and stated objectives must be formally documented, escalated, and remediated — that is the entire purpose of testing. The correct process: document the finding in the test report; assign ownership; analyse options (pre-warm the server, optimise startup scripts, upgrade hardware, adjust RTO with business acceptance); implement the chosen remediation; re-test to validate the fix. Accepting the gap (A) defeats the test's purpose. Arbitrarily extending RTOs (C) without business approval violates BCP governance.
7Which of the following BEST describes the difference between a Business Continuity Plan (BCP) and a Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)?
✅ Correct: B. BCP is the umbrella plan covering all aspects of business continuity: people, processes, communications, facilities, manual workarounds, vendor relationships, and IT recovery. DRP is a subset of BCP that specifically addresses IT infrastructure restoration: servers, applications, databases, networks. All DRPs are part of a BCP, but not all BCP activities are DRP activities. Manual business procedure continuity (paper-based processing, manual customer service) is BCP but not DRP. CISSP tests this distinction regularly.
8A major retailer's data centre suffers a catastrophic failure. The CTO's first instinct is to immediately contact all media outlets with a public statement. The Chief Legal Officer strongly advises against this. Which crisis communications principle BEST supports the CLO's position?
✅ Correct: B. Effective crisis communications requires: a designated single spokesperson (prevents conflicting messages); pre-planned templates and approval processes; legal/PR review before external publication; prioritised notification sequence (employees first, then customers, then regulators, then media); factual and honest messaging without speculation. Immediate uncontrolled media statements (A proposal in the question) can mislead, create liability, or make the situation worse. Withholding all communication (C) leaves stakeholders without needed information and creates secondary crises around trust.
9An organisation's BCP was last updated 3 years ago. Since then, the company has migrated 70% of its infrastructure to the cloud and acquired two new business units. What triggered set MOST requires an immediate BCP review?
✅ Correct: B. BCPs must be updated when: significant infrastructure changes occur (cloud migration 70% of infrastructure = radically different recovery procedures); organisational changes happen (acquisitions introduce new systems, people, processes, and regulatory obligations); test findings reveal gaps; incidents expose weaknesses; or the annual review is due. A 3-year-old BCP in a company that has undergone major transformation is not just outdated — it is dangerous, providing false confidence that a viable plan exists when the documented procedures no longer reflect reality.
10Under software escrow in a BCP context, what is the PRIMARY purpose of depositing source code with a third-party escrow agent?
✅ Correct: B. Software escrow protects the customer from vendor failure risk. If the vendor goes bankrupt, is acquired, or discontinues the product, the customer needs access to source code to maintain, modify, or migrate the application. The escrow agent holds an up-to-date copy of the source code (updated at agreed intervals) and releases it to the customer only upon trigger events defined in the agreement. This is a supply chain continuity control addressing vendor dependency risk — directly relevant to BCP strategy for organisations dependent on critical third-party software.
