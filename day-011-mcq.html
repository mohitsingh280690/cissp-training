1A CISO must rapidly assess security risks across 15 newly acquired business units. Historical financial loss data is unavailable for most of them, and the assessment must be completed within two weeks. Which risk analysis approach is MOST appropriate?
✅ Correct: B. Qualitative risk analysis uses expert judgment, interviews, and ordinal rating scales (High/Medium/Low, 1–5) rather than financial data. It is faster, requires less data, and can be applied consistently across diverse environments. Its key advantages in this scenario: speed (two-week deadline), breadth (15 units), and no dependency on historical loss data. Quantitative analysis's accuracy requires months of data gathering. Deferral (D) is not a valid risk management response when the organisation is already exposed.
2Two risks are scored on a 5×5 qualitative risk matrix. Risk A: Likelihood 4, Impact 2 (score = 8). Risk B: Likelihood 2, Impact 4 (score = 8). A junior analyst argues they should receive identical treatment since they have the same score. What is a fundamental limitation of risk matrices this scenario illustrates?
✅ Correct: B. This is a well-documented limitation of risk matrices called "risk score conflation." Risk A (frequent, minor impact) is best addressed by operational controls that reduce frequency. Risk B (rare, catastrophic impact) is best addressed by consequence-reduction controls and contingency planning. Treating them identically based on equal scores leads to misallocation of resources. Risk A requires different treatment (frequency reduction) than Risk B (impact reduction/transfer). Good risk practitioners look beyond the score to the underlying likelihood/impact profile.
3A risk assessment team uses a technique where each expert submits risk ratings independently and anonymously. The facilitator aggregates results and shares them with the group without revealing who said what. Experts revise ratings based on the summary, and the process repeats until consensus emerges. This technique is called:
✅ Correct: C. The Delphi Technique is an iterative, structured, anonymous expert consensus process. Key features: anonymous inputs prevent group dynamics from biasing results; facilitator aggregates and shares summaries (not individual responses); multiple rounds allow convergence. It was developed by RAND Corporation for forecasting. In security risk assessment, it is particularly valuable for sensitive topics where participants might not speak freely in open forums. The Nominal Group Technique (B) involves structured silent idea generation followed by voting — it's not iterative.
4During risk matrix review, a risk scores 3 (Likelihood) × 1 (Impact) = 3 out of 25. Another risk scores 1 (Likelihood) × 5 (Impact) = 5 out of 25. Risk management policy treats any risk with Impact ≥ 5 as a priority regardless of overall score. Why is this policy sound?
✅ Correct: C. Risk matrices can underweight catastrophic but rare risks. A score of 5 from a catastrophic event (1×5) may be lower than a medium risk score (3×3=9) — but the catastrophic event could be existential (enterprise failure, mass casualty, critical infrastructure destruction). Many risk frameworks establish "bright line" rules that escalate any risk scoring the maximum impact severity regardless of its total risk score. This prevents "black swan" risks from being buried under numerically higher but less severe risks. CISSP candidates should recognise when impact alone justifies priority treatment.
5An organisation's risk matrix defines "High" impact as "disrupts primary business operations for more than 72 hours." A junior analyst rates a vulnerability as "High" impact because it "could be bad." A senior analyst disagrees, saying the impact should be "Medium." What is the MOST important control to prevent this inconsistency?
✅ Correct: A. The most critical design requirement for qualitative risk scales is anchored definitions — each rating level must be defined with specific, observable criteria (e.g., "High = business disruption >72h; financial impact >$1M; regulatory fine certain"). Without anchors, ratings reflect assessors' subjective interpretation rather than objective conditions, making comparisons across assessors and time periods invalid. This is a well-known weakness of qualitative methods — it is addressed through well-designed rating rubrics, calibration workshops, and consensus reviews.
6The Delphi Technique's anonymous submission process is specifically designed to address which cognitive and group dynamics challenge in risk assessment workshops?
✅ Correct: B. The HiPPO Effect (Highest Paid Person's Opinion) describes the tendency in group settings for participants to defer to authority figures — even when they have better information. Groupthink is the broader phenomenon where conformity pressures suppress dissenting views. Delphi's anonymity breaks both: experts cannot be identified, so seniority, politics, and personality cannot distort the aggregated view. This is the fundamental reason for Delphi's design — it extracts true expert knowledge uncontaminated by social pressure. The CISSP exam tests awareness of these cognitive biases.
7A risk workshop is conducted where the CISO presents his risk ratings first before other participants share their views. Several participants who initially disagreed privately end up adopting the CISO's ratings after hearing them. This scenario illustrates which specific cognitive bias?
✅ Correct: B. Anchoring bias occurs when the first information presented disproportionately influences subsequent judgments — all subsequent estimates "anchor" to the initial value. When combined with the HiPPO Effect (deference to authority), participants who hear the CISO's rating first are doubly biased: they anchor to it and defer to it socially. This illustrates why independent, anonymous initial ratings (as in Delphi) produce more accurate risk assessments than open voting after a senior person stakes a position.
8Which of the following is a valid ADVANTAGE of qualitative risk analysis compared to quantitative analysis for an organisation with diverse, rapidly changing IT assets?
✅ Correct: B. Qualitative analysis advantages: faster execution (hours/days vs. weeks/months), no historical data requirement, accessible to non-financial stakeholders, easily repeatable, produces actionable risk priority rankings. Its primary disadvantage is the inability to directly calculate ROI or CBA for control investments. Many organisations use a hybrid approach: qualitative for broad assessment and prioritisation, then quantitative for the top-priority risks to support budget decisions. ISO 27001 accepts both approaches.
9Which risk assessment methodology was developed by Carnegie Mellon's Software Engineering Institute, is self-directed by the organisation's own staff, and uses workshop-based asset-centric threat profiling without requiring external facilitators or financial calculations?
✅ Correct: B. OCTAVE was developed by Carnegie Mellon's CERT/SEI. The OCTAVE family includes: OCTAVE (original, large organisations), OCTAVE-S (small organisations), and OCTAVE Allegro (streamlined, information-asset focused). All versions are self-directed — performed by internal staff using structured worksheets — and are qualitative, focusing on identifying critical assets, threats, and vulnerabilities without financial modelling. CISSP candidates regularly encounter OCTAVE in qualitative methodology questions.
10After completing a qualitative risk assessment that prioritises 20 risks as High/Medium/Low, the board asks for dollar figures to justify next year's security budget. What is the MOST appropriate follow-on action?
✅ Correct: D. The optimal hybrid approach uses qualitative analysis to rapidly prioritise risks, then applies quantitative analysis to the highest-priority subset to generate the financial figures needed for CBA and budget justification. This is efficient: you avoid the cost and time of quantifying all 20 risks and focus rigorous financial modelling where it matters most. Arbitrarily assigning dollars to qualitative scales (B) corrupts the analysis with false precision. This hybrid methodology is increasingly adopted in enterprise risk management frameworks including NIST RMF.
