7. Common Exam Traps from Mock 1

TrapWhy Candidates Fall for ItHow to Avoid
"Most secure" answerCandidates pick the most technically restrictive optionCISSP asks for BEST answer considering business context — the most restrictive isn't always best if it kills business operations
Technical vs managerial answerCandidates pick the technical "fix" instead of the governance solutionWhen two answers seem correct, pick the managerial/policy/governance answer first. CISSP is a management exam
"First" vs "most important"Different question types need different approaches"FIRST" = sequence (what comes before); "MOST IMPORTANT" = priority (what matters most regardless of sequence)
Absolute wordsAnswers with "always," "never," "all," "none" seem definitiveAbsolute answers are usually WRONG on CISSP. Security is about risk management, not absolutes
Technology-specific answersVendor/product names seem authoritativeCISSP is vendor-neutral. Answers with specific product names are usually distractors
Scope creep in answersAn answer that addresses more than the question asksPick the answer that DIRECTLY addresses exactly what was asked — no more, no less

8. "Think Like a Manager" Decision Framework

The CISSP Mindset: You are a CISO advising the board, not a system administrator fixing servers. Every decision must balance security with business needs, legal requirements, and available resources.

Decision Priority Hierarchy

PriorityPrincipleExample
1Human safety FIRSTEvacuate before fighting fire; unlock doors during emergency even if security is reduced
2Legal/regulatory complianceNotify regulators within required timeframes; comply with court orders; follow privacy laws
3Prevent further damageContain the incident; stop data loss; isolate compromised systems
4Restore operationsRecover from backups; bring systems back online; resume business functions
5Investigate and improveRoot cause analysis; lessons learned; process improvements

When Two Answers Both Seem Correct

  • Policy before technology: Governance/policy answer beats technical control answer
  • Prevent before detect: Preventive control beats detective control (unless the question specifically asks about detection)
  • Address root cause before symptom: Fixing the underlying issue beats patching the immediate problem
  • People before process before technology: Training/awareness beats procedure change beats technical control
  • Risk-based over checklist-based: Risk assessment answer beats compliance-checkbox answer
