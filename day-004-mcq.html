1A prosecutor brings cybercrime charges against a hacker who compromised a financial institution's servers. What standard of proof must be met for a criminal conviction?
✅ Correct: C. Criminal law requires proof "beyond reasonable doubt" — the highest evidentiary standard. This reflects the severity of consequences (imprisonment, criminal record). Civil law uses "preponderance of evidence" (~51%). Clear and convincing evidence (~75%) appears in some civil and administrative proceedings. Knowing these three standards and which context each applies to is a CISSP exam staple.
2A terminated employee continues to access their former employer's systems using credentials that were not promptly deactivated. Under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA), this access is MOST likely considered:
✅ Correct: B. The CFAA (18 U.S.C. § 1030) prohibits unauthorized access to protected computers. Upon termination, authorization to access the system ends — credential persistence does not constitute authorization. The CFAA provides for both civil (private lawsuits) and criminal (DOJ prosecution) remedies. Option D is a dangerous misconception: authorization is determined by employment status, not by whether IT remembered to revoke credentials.
3A customer whose credit card data was stolen in a breach sues the company for negligence. The plaintiff must establish four elements. Which element requires proving that the security failure was the PROXIMATE CAUSE of the harm, not merely one of many contributing factors?
✅ Correct: C. Causation (specifically proximate cause) is often the most contested element in cybersecurity negligence cases. Defendants frequently argue that the attacker's sophistication, not the defendant's failure, was the proximate cause. Establishing that the security failure directly led to the harm — rather than being one of several contributing factors — is the key causation challenge. The four negligence elements are: Duty → Breach → Causation → Damages (mnemonic: "Duty Broken Causes Damage").
4The Budapest Convention on Cybercrime (Council of Europe Convention 185) is significant in international cybersecurity law primarily because it:
✅ Correct: B. The Budapest Convention provides a framework for international cooperation: harmonising criminal laws on cybercrime, establishing investigative procedures, and enabling mutual legal assistance (MLA) between signatory nations. It does NOT create an international court (A), prohibit state-sponsored cyber operations (C), or apply to all UN members — only the 68+ signatories (D is wrong because it says "all 193 UN members").
5A US company discovers a cyberattack originated from servers in a country that has not signed the Budapest Convention and with which the US has no bilateral cybercrime treaty. What is the MOST significant legal challenge this creates?
✅ Correct: B. The primary challenge is practical: without treaty obligations, the host country has no legal obligation to cooperate, extradite, or share evidence. The US may still assert jurisdiction (protective or nationality principles) and charge the attackers — but actually apprehending them or obtaining evidence requires that country's cooperation. Options C and D overstate the limitation; US law can still apply, but enforcement is severely constrained.
6A company's security team wants to use server access logs as evidence in a civil lawsuit. Under the Federal Rules of Evidence Business Records Exception, which condition MOST critically ensures admissibility of these logs?
✅ Correct: B. The Business Records Exception to hearsay (FRE Rule 803(6)) requires: records kept in the regular course of business; by someone with knowledge of the events; as a regular practice of the business. Option D is the exact opposite of what the exception requires — records created specifically for litigation do NOT qualify for this exception (they may be self-serving). This is why routine, automated log generation is more legally defensible than manually assembled logs.
7A nation asserts the right to prosecute cybercrimes committed by its own citizens regardless of where in the world those crimes were committed. This is known as which basis of jurisdiction?
✅ Correct: D. Nationality (active personality) jurisdiction allows a state to prosecute its own nationals for crimes committed abroad. This is distinct from territorial (where it happened), protective (protecting national security interests), and passive personality (prosecuting offenses against the state's nationals abroad). The US uses nationality jurisdiction for some cybercrime cases involving US citizens committing crimes against foreign entities.
8Which type of law governs disputes between private parties where the typical remedy is monetary compensation rather than incarceration?
✅ Correct: C. Civil/Tort law governs disputes between private parties (person vs. person, person vs. company, company vs. company). The typical remedy is monetary damages. Criminal law involves state prosecution with imprisonment as a possible remedy. Administrative law involves regulatory agencies. The distinction between civil, criminal, and administrative is fundamental for the CISSP exam's legal topics.
9A security analyst detects an active intrusion from an identified external attacker. A colleague suggests launching a retaliatory "hack-back" attack to shut down the attacker's systems. Under the CFAA, the MOST accurate legal assessment is:
✅ Correct: B. The CFAA (18 U.S.C. § 1030) prohibits unauthorized access to computer systems. Hack-back means accessing systems you don't own without authorization — and that is a federal crime regardless of whether you're "retaliating." There is no self-defense exception in the CFAA. Additionally, attackers frequently use compromised systems ("bounce points") as intermediaries, meaning hack-back could harm innocent third-party victims. The Active Cyber Defense Certainty Act proposed but has NOT been enacted as law.
10An organization receives a notice from the FTC threatening administrative fines for alleged unfair data privacy practices. This situation falls under which category of law?
✅ Correct: C. Administrative/Regulatory law involves government agencies exercising delegated legislative authority to establish and enforce rules. The FTC, FCC, SEC, HIPAA via HHS, and similar agencies are administrative bodies. Their enforcement actions — including fines and consent decrees — are administrative law proceedings. These are distinct from criminal prosecutions (beyond reasonable doubt, jail time) and civil tort suits (private plaintiff, monetary damages).
