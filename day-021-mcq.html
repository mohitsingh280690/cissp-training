Day 21 – Information & Asset Classification

MCQ Practice – Domain 2: Asset Security

Question 1: Who is primarily responsible for determining the classification level of a data asset?

✅ Correct: C. The Data Owner — typically senior management or the business function head — is responsible for determining the classification of data. The data owner understands the business context, regulatory obligations, and sensitivity of the information. The custodian implements controls; the security team advises; users follow the policies.

Question 2: A company has just completed a merger that was previously classified as Confidential. The merger has now been publicly announced. What should happen to those documents?

✅ Correct: B. When information no longer meets the criteria that justified its original classification (the merger is now public), the data owner should evaluate whether declassification or reclassification to a lower level is appropriate. Only the data owner can approve this change (not the custodian). Classification may be reduced when the underlying sensitivity criteria no longer apply.

Question 3: Which statement BEST describes the relationship between asset classification and security controls?

✅ Correct: B. Classification level drives control selection — higher classification requires stronger controls (stronger encryption, stricter access control, more rigorous handling procedures, secure destruction). This is the fundamental purpose of classification: enable proportionate, risk-based application of security controls. Applying uniform controls to all data ignores risk proportionality and is both wasteful and ineffective.

Question 4: The IT team backs up a classified database nightly, applies encryption, and tests restoration quarterly. This is BEST described as the activity of which role?

✅ Correct: D. The Data Custodian (typically IT operations) implements and maintains the technical controls that the classification requires — including backups, encryption, patch management, and restoration testing. They don't decide what level of protection is needed (that's the owner); they execute it.

Question 5: An organisation discovers a large database of customer records on an unregistered cloud storage bucket. Which principle does this MOST directly violate?

✅ Correct: B. An unregistered (undiscovered) asset cannot be classified, protected, or included in security policies. This is the fundamental failure of asset inventory management — "shadow IT" represents assets outside the governance framework. The principle "you cannot protect what you don't know you have" is the foundational reason for maintaining comprehensive asset inventories.

Question 6: Which factor would MOST likely cause an organisation to INCREASE the classification level of existing data?

✅ Correct: B. Reclassification upward (increased classification) is triggered when circumstances increase the sensitivity of the data — such as a new regulation that now classifies previously unregulated data as PII or PHI, requiring heightened protection. Age (A) typically reduces sensitivity; access requests (C) don't determine classification; clean audits (D) reflect controls, not classification level.

Question 7: Which of the following criteria is LEAST relevant when determining the initial classification of a data asset?

✅ Correct: C. File size and storage format (CSV, JSON, database) have no bearing on the sensitivity or classification of data. A small 1KB file could contain a nation's nuclear codes; a 1TB video archive might be fully public. What matters for classification is the sensitivity, regulatory classification, competitive value, and privacy impact of the data's CONTENT — not its technical container.

Question 8: At what step in the classification process should the data owner FIRST become involved?

✅ Correct: B. The data owner's first involvement in the classification process is at the ownership assignment and sensitivity evaluation step — they are the subject matter expert on the business context and value of the data. Once ownership is confirmed, the owner evaluates sensitivity criteria and assigns the classification. Technical implementation (A, C) is done by custodians afterward; audit review (D) is an ongoing monitoring activity.

Question 9: An organisation classifies all customer data as "Confidential" by default during onboarding. This approach is BEST described as:

✅ Correct: B. Defaulting to a higher classification level during onboarding (before formal review) is a prudent approach — it protects potentially sensitive data with appropriate controls while the formal classification process is completed. This is better than defaulting to an unclassified or public default. The classification can be adjusted after formal review. This is the principle of "classify early, review often."

Question 10: Why is the asset inventory considered the STARTING POINT for asset classification?

✅ Correct: B. You cannot classify assets you don't know exist. The asset inventory is the comprehensive list that captures all data, systems, applications, and assets in scope for classification. Without it, assets fall through the cracks — undiscovered assets receive no classification, no ownership assignment, and no controls. The inventory is the prerequisite for every subsequent classification and governance activity.
