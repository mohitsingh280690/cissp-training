20-Question MCQ – Domain 1: Security and Risk Management
📋 Instructions: 20 CISSP-style questions covering all of Domain 1. Select one answer per question. Click Submit All Answers to see your score and explanations. Pass mark: 14/20 (70%).

Question 1: A server has an AV of $200,000, an EF of 50%, and the threat occurs 0.25 times per year. What is the ALE?

✅ Correct: A. SLE = AV × EF = $200,000 × 0.50 = $100,000. ALE = SLE × ARO = $100,000 × 0.25 = $25,000 per year.

Question 2: An organisation's MTD for a critical application is 24 hours. Its RTO is set at 18 hours. What is the MAXIMUM allowable WRT?

✅ Correct: C. RTO + WRT ≤ MTD. WRT ≤ MTD − RTO = 24 − 18 = 6 hours. The WRT cannot exceed 6 hours without violating the MTD constraint.

Question 3: Which security model focuses on INTEGRITY and uses the principles "no write up, no read down"?

✅ Correct: B. The Biba model protects integrity: subjects cannot write to higher-integrity objects (no write up) and cannot read from lower-integrity objects (no read down). Bell-LaPadula protects confidentiality (no read up, no write down). Clark-Wilson uses transaction controls for integrity. Brewer-Nash addresses conflict of interest.

Question 4: Management decides to accept risk after reviewing risk assessment results. What documentation requirement MUST accompany this decision?

✅ Correct: B. Risk acceptance must be formally documented and signed off by management. This distinguishes "accepting" risk (conscious, documented decision) from "ignoring" risk (no decision, no documentation). The documentation demonstrates due care — management acknowledged the risk and consciously chose to bear it.

Question 5: A system has an MTBF of 900 hours and an MTTR of 100 hours. What is its availability?

✅ Correct: A. Availability = MTBF ÷ (MTBF + MTTR) = 900 ÷ (900 + 100) = 900 ÷ 1000 = 0.90 = 90%.

Question 6: Which of the following represents the policy hierarchy in DESCENDING order of authority (highest to lowest)?

✅ Correct: B. The hierarchy from highest authority/broadest to lowest/most specific: Policy (management intent) → Standards (mandatory requirements) → Guidelines (recommended practices, optional) → Procedures (step-by-step instructions). Standards are mandatory; guidelines are not — this is a frequent exam distinction.

Question 7: An organisation wants to transfer risk for cyber incidents. Which action BEST achieves this?

✅ Correct: C. Risk transfer shifts the financial consequences of a risk to a third party (usually an insurance company). Cyber liability insurance is the primary mechanism for risk transfer in cybersecurity. MFA (A) is risk mitigation (reducing probability). Discontinuing the process (B) is risk avoidance. Document acceptance (D) is risk acceptance.

Question 8: What is the PRIMARY difference between due care and due diligence?

✅ Correct: C. Due care means taking reasonable, appropriate actions to protect assets — the doing. Due diligence means continuously researching, investigating, and monitoring to understand what those actions should be — the knowing. Option B reverses the definitions. They are NOT interchangeable and appear on the exam as a classic distinction test.

Question 9: A business continuity plan test involves shutting down production systems and fully activating the disaster recovery site. This is BEST described as:

✅ Correct: C. Full interruption test (also called full-scale test) is the most disruptive but most realistic BCP test — production is shut down and the recovery site must handle real operations. A parallel test activates the recovery site WHILE production stays up. A tabletop exercise is a discussion only. A simulation involves some system participation but not full failover.

Question 10: Under GDPR, an organisation must report a qualifying personal data breach to the supervisory authority within:

✅ Correct: B. GDPR Article 33 requires notification to the supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a qualifying personal data breach. If notification cannot be made within 72 hours, the reasons for the delay must be reported. Data subjects must also be notified "without undue delay" when the breach is likely to result in high risk to their rights and freedoms.

Question 11: Which IP protection mechanism BEST protects a company's customer database schema and proprietary algorithms that have never been publicly disclosed?

✅ Correct: D. Trade secrets protect confidential business information that derives value from not being publicly known and is subject to reasonable secrecy precautions. There is no registration requirement — protection depends on maintaining secrecy. Patents require public disclosure. Copyright protects expression, not underlying methods. Trademarks protect brand identity.

Question 12: Which framework uses the Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover lifecycle and is widely used as a voluntary cybersecurity framework by US and international organisations?

✅ Correct: C. The NIST CSF uses five functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover. It is voluntary, risk-based, and widely adopted. ISO 27001 uses Plan-Do-Check-Act and is certifiable. COBIT focuses on IT governance (goals, enablers). SABSA is a security architecture framework.

Question 13: A security policy states: "All remote access connections must use VPN with AES-256 encryption." This document is BEST classified as:

✅ Correct: C. A standard is a mandatory, specific, measurable requirement — "MUST use VPN with AES-256" defines a required specification. A policy would say something like "Remote access shall be secured appropriately" (what, not how specifically). A guideline would say "Consider using VPN with strong encryption" (recommended, not required). A procedure would provide step-by-step instructions on how to set up the VPN.

Question 14: A warm site recovery option was selected for a business continuity plan. A disaster occurs. Which statement BEST describes the warm site's readiness?

✅ Correct: B. A warm site has hardware pre-staged and configured but requires restoration of data from backup media before it is fully operational — typically taking 1–7 days. A hot site (A) has live mirrored data and can fail over in hours. A cold site (C) is just a facility with power; no pre-staged equipment. Parallel operation (D) would describe a redundant active-active architecture, not a standard recovery site type.

Question 15: Which type of security policy MUST be complied with based on regulatory or legal requirements and carries legal penalties for non-compliance?

✅ Correct: C. A regulatory policy is required to comply with applicable laws and regulations (HIPAA, PCI-DSS, GDPR, SOX). Non-compliance carries regulatory penalties, fines, or legal liability. Advisory policies strongly recommend behaviour but have no external legal mandate. Informative policies provide information and education. Issue-specific policies address specific security topics within an organisation.

Question 16: The ISC² Code of Ethics canon that takes precedence over all others in case of conflict is:

✅ Correct: B. The ISC² Code of Ethics has four canons in priority order. Canon 1 — "Protect society, the common good, necessary public infrastructure, and the global information infrastructure" — takes precedence when canons conflict. This means CISSP holders have an obligation to society above even client obligations in crisis situations. The exam occasionally asks about this ordering.

Question 17: An organisation discovers a software vendor it relies on has not patched a critical CVE in their product for six months. Which risk management concept BEST describes this situation?

✅ Correct: B. Supply chain risk arises from dependencies on external vendors and third parties. An organisation's security posture is only as strong as its supply chain. A vendor's failure to patch a known vulnerability creates risk in all organisations that depend on that product — this is a supply chain risk management issue. SCRM (Supply Chain Risk Management) includes vendor assessment, contractual security requirements, and ongoing vendor monitoring.

Question 18: Which personnel control BEST addresses the risk of a single administrator having unchecked ability to both create and approve changes to production systems?

✅ Correct: C. Separation of Duties prevents a single person from having end-to-end control of a sensitive process. The administrator should be able to create changes OR approve them — not both. This is a classic SoD control in change management and matches the "two-person integrity" principle. Least privilege limits what they can access, but SoD addresses the more specific risk of unchecked single-person authority over a complete process.

Question 19: A security awareness programme's phishing simulation shows a 40% click rate in January, 20% in April, and 9% in October. What can be concluded about this data?

✅ Correct: B. A declining click rate from 40% to 9% over three measurement periods demonstrates clear, measurable behavioural improvement — this is exactly what a successful security awareness programme should show. No programme achieves 0% (humans remain fallible), so 9% at year-end is a significant success. The data trend is valuable for demonstrating ROI to management and continuing programme funding.

Question 20: Which of the following BEST describes the relationship between risk appetite and risk tolerance?

✅ Correct: B. Risk appetite is the broad, strategic declaration of how much risk the organisation is willing to accept in pursuit of its objectives — set at the board/executive level. Risk tolerance is the acceptable deviation around that appetite — the practical operational boundaries within which the organisation can function without escalation. Example: risk appetite = "low risk in data handling"; risk tolerance = "no more than 0.1% of customer records exposed per quarter."
