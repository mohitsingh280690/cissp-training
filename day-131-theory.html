1. Domain 1 – Security & Risk Management (16%)

The LARGEST domain by exam weight. Covers foundational security principles, governance, risk, compliance, legal/regulatory, ethics, and BCP.

Core Security Concepts — CIA + Beyond

PrincipleDefinitionThreat

IntegrityPrevent unauthorized modificationTampering, malware, bit rot
AvailabilityEnsure timely reliable accessDoS, ransomware, disasters
AuthenticityVerify identity/originSpoofing, impersonation
Non-repudiationCannot deny actionRepudiation of transactions

Security Governance

DocumentLevelDescription

ProcedureOperationalMandatory; step-by-step instructions

Risk Management

ConceptKey Formula/Definition
RiskThreat × Vulnerability × Impact (or Asset Value)
SLESingle Loss Expectancy = Asset Value × Exposure Factor
ALEAnnual Loss Expectancy = SLE × ARO
Risk responsesMitigate, Transfer, Accept, Avoid (MTAA)
Residual riskRisk remaining AFTER controls; must be formally accepted by management
Risk appetiteAmount of risk org is WILLING to accept
Risk toleranceAcceptable variation from risk appetite
Quantitative vs Qualitative Risk Analysis:
• Quantitative = numbers, money (SLE, ALE, ARO) — more objective
• Qualitative = categories (High/Medium/Low) — faster, more subjective
• Most organizations use BOTH; start with qualitative, then quantitative for high-priority risks

Law/RegulationFocus

BCP/BIA

ConceptKey Point
BIAIdentifies critical business functions and impact of disruption; FIRST step in BCP
MTD/MADMaximum Tolerable Downtime — longest a function can be down before catastrophic damage
RTORecovery Time Objective — target time to restore function; RTO ≤ MTD
RPORecovery Point Objective — max acceptable data loss; drives backup frequency
WRTWork Recovery Time — time to verify/catch up after restoration; RTO + WRT ≤ MTD

Data Roles

RoleResponsibility
Data OwnerSenior management; classifies data; defines access policy; accountable for data protection
Data CustodianIT operations; implements controls defined by owner; manages backups, encryption, storage
Data StewardEnsures data quality, metadata management, compliance with data governance policies
Data ProcessorThird party that processes data on behalf of the data controller (GDPR term)
Data ControllerDetermines purposes and means of processing personal data (GDPR term)
Data SubjectIndividual whose personal data is being processed

Data Lifecycle

PhaseSecurity Consideration
Create/CollectClassify at creation; minimize collection; consent
StoreEncrypt at rest; access controls; backups
UseLeast privilege; DLP; monitoring
Share/TransferEncrypt in transit; DLP; data agreements
ArchiveLong-term storage; retention policies; crypto agility
DestroyCrypto-shredding; degaussing; physical destruction; verify sanitization

Data Destruction Methods

MethodMediaDescription
OverwritingHDD/SSDWrite patterns over data; multiple passes for HDD; may not reach all SSD blocks
DegaussingMagnetic media only (HDD, tape)Powerful magnetic field destroys data; does NOT work on SSD/flash
Crypto-shreddingEncrypted mediaDestroy encryption key → data unrecoverable; fast; works on any encrypted media
Physical destructionAll typesShredding, incineration, pulverizing; most definitive
Degaussing does NOT work on SSDs/flash drives. SSDs store data electronically, not magnetically. For SSD destruction: crypto-shredding or physical destruction. This is a frequently tested exam point.

Privacy Principles

PrincipleDescription
Data minimizationCollect only what's needed for the stated purpose
Purpose limitationUse data only for the purpose it was collected for
ConsentObtain explicit or informed consent before collection/processing
Right to accessIndividuals can request access to their personal data
Right to erasureIndividuals can request deletion of their data (GDPR "right to be forgotten")
Data protection by designPrivacy built into system design, not added later (GDPR Article 25)

4. Master Comparison Tables

Risk Responses

ResponseActionExample
Mitigate/ReduceImplement controls to reduce likelihood or impactInstall firewall, encrypt data
Transfer/ShareShift risk to third partyInsurance, outsourcing, SLA
AcceptAcknowledge risk and bear it (cost of control > loss)Document acceptance; management signs off
AvoidEliminate the risk by eliminating the activityDon't collect SSNs; don't enter that market

Control Types

CategoryFunction
PreventiveStop incident before it occurs (firewall, access control, training)
DetectiveIdentify incident during/after (IDS, audit logs, SIEM)
CorrectiveFix/restore after incident (patching, BCP activation, IPS blocking)
DeterrentDiscourage attempt (warning banners, cameras, guards)
CompensatingAlternative when primary control not feasible
RecoveryRestore to normal operations (backups, DR site)

5. High-Yield Exam Concepts

#ConceptExam Point
1Due care vs due diligenceDue care = DO the right thing; Due diligence = VERIFY/RESEARCH it's the right thing
2ALE calculationALE = SLE × ARO; SLE = AV × EF; if control costs less than ALE reduction, implement it
3Data owner vs custodianOwner = senior mgmt, classifies, sets policy; Custodian = IT, implements controls
4Residual risk acceptanceManagement MUST formally accept residual risk
5GDPR 72-hour breach notificationReport to supervisory authority within 72 hours; data subjects "without undue delay"
6Degaussing vs SSDDegaussing doesn't work on SSD — use crypto-shredding or physical destruction
7RPO drives backup frequencyRPO = 4 hours means backup at least every 4 hours
8Policy = mandatory; Guideline = optionalOnly guidelines are NOT mandatory
9ISC2 Code of EthicsProtect society → Act honorably → Provide service → Advance the profession
10Data remanenceData remaining after deletion; sanitization required for media reuse
