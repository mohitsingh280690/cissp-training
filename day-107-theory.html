2. Real-World Organizational Relevance

Threat intelligence transforms security from reactive to proactive. Instead of waiting for attacks, organizations can anticipate threats, pre-position defenses, and reduce attacker dwell time. The 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack was first discovered by FireEye through its own internal monitoring; the resulting shared threat intelligence then helped thousands of other affected organizations detect and respond to the compromise.

5. Indicators of Compromise (IoC)

IoC Types — The Pyramid of Pain

David Bianco's Pyramid of Pain ranks IoC types by how much pain they cause the attacker when detected and blocked:

Level (Bottom → Top)IoC TypeAttacker PainDefender Effort
1 (easiest to change)Hash values (file hashes)Trivial — recompile and get new hashLow — simple blocklist
2IP addressesEasy — use new infrastructureLow — firewall rules
3Domain namesSimple — register new domainsLow-Medium — DNS blocklist
4Network/host artifactsAnnoying — must change tools and payloadsMedium — behavioral rules
5ToolsChallenging — must find or develop new toolsMedium-High — tool detection
6 (hardest to change)TTPs (Tactics, Techniques, Procedures)Tough — must fundamentally change methodologyHigh — behavioral analytics, UEBA
Pyramid of Pain — Exam Insight: Detecting and blocking TTPs (the top of the pyramid) is the most effective defense because it forces adversaries to completely change their operational methodology — which is expensive and time-consuming. This is why frameworks like MITRE ATT&CK (which catalogs TTPs) are so valuable.

ModelDescription
CollectionConsumers poll a server to retrieve intelligence (pull model)

Exam Tip: STIX = the format/language (WHAT you share). TAXII = the transport mechanism (HOW you share it). Together they enable automated, machine-to-machine threat intelligence sharing.

7. MITRE ATT&CK Framework

MITRE ATT&CK: A comprehensive, publicly available knowledge base of adversary tactics and techniques based on real-world observations. ATT&CK stands for Adversarial Tactics, Techniques, and Common Knowledge.

ATT&CK Structure

LevelDescriptionExample
TacticThe adversary's objective — the WHY (14 categories in Enterprise ATT&CK)Initial Access; Execution; Persistence; Privilege Escalation; Defense Evasion; Credential Access; Discovery; Lateral Movement; Collection; Command and Control; Exfiltration; Impact
TechniqueHOW the adversary achieves the tacticUnder "Initial Access": Phishing (T1566), Exploit Public-Facing Application (T1190), Drive-by Compromise (T1189)
Sub-techniqueMore specific variant of a techniqueUnder Phishing: Spearphishing Attachment (T1566.001), Spearphishing Link (T1566.002)
ProcedureSpecific implementation by a known groupAPT28 uses spearphishing with macro-enabled Word documents

ATT&CK Use Cases

  • Detection engineering: Map SIEM rules to ATT&CK techniques to identify coverage gaps
  • Red/purple teaming: Use ATT&CK to plan realistic adversary simulations
  • Threat assessment: Identify which techniques are most relevant to your industry/adversaries
  • IR documentation: Describe incidents using standardized technique IDs
ATT&CK vs Kill Chain: Lockheed Martin Cyber Kill Chain (7 linear stages) describes the attack lifecycle. ATT&CK is more granular and non-linear — adversaries do not follow a fixed sequence. ATT&CK provides specific techniques for each tactic, making it more actionable for detection engineering.

8. Threat Intelligence Platforms & ISACs

Threat Intelligence Platform (TIP)

TIP: A software platform that aggregates, correlates, and manages threat intelligence from multiple sources (OSINT, commercial, government, internal). TIPs normalize data into a common format, deduplicate, and distribute intelligence to consumers (SIEM, firewalls, EDR).

Information Sharing and Analysis Centers (ISACs)

ISACSector
FS-ISACFinancial Services
H-ISACHealthcare
E-ISAC / ES-ISACElectricity / Energy
MS-ISACMulti-State (government)
IT-ISACInformation Technology
A-ISACAviation

ISACs are sector-specific organizations that facilitate sharing of threat intelligence and best practices among member organizations. They provide anonymized incident reports, early warnings, and analysis of sector-targeted threats. ISAOs (Information Sharing and Analysis Organizations) are similar but not sector-restricted.

9. Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🧠 Intelligence Levels — "STOT"
Strategic (executives — trends)
Tactical (managers — TTPs)
Operational (IR leads — campaigns)
Technical (SOC/tools — IoCs)
"Strategy before Tactics before Operations before Technical details"
🧠 STIX vs TAXII
STIX = eXpression (the language)
TAXII = taXi cab (the transport)
"STIX speaks it, TAXII ships it"
🧠 Threat Intel Lifecycle — "PCP-ADF"
Planning → Collection → Processing → Analysis → Dissemination → Feedback
"Plan, Collect, Process, Analyze, Disseminate, Feedback"
🧠 Pyramid of Pain — Bottom to Top
Hashes → IPs → Domains → Artifacts → Tools → TTPs
"HIDATT — Hide the ATTacker by blocking what hurts them most (TTPs)"
