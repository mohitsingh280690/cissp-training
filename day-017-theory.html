2. Real-World Organizational Relevance

BCP strategy choices have direct financial implications. A hot site costs 3–5× more than a cold site but reduces RTO from days to hours. Organisations with 4-hour RTOs cannot afford cold sites — the BIA justifies the investment premium. Real-world BCP failures often occur not because plans don't exist, but because they were never tested, are outdated, or key personnel don't know their roles during a crisis.

Crisis communications failures during incidents (poor stakeholder communication, delayed notification) consistently rank among the most damaging reputational outcomes of disasters — often worse than the technical failure itself. The 2017 British Airways IT outage cost over £80M largely due to botched crisis communication alongside the technical failure.

3. BCP Strategy Overview

BCP strategy is selected after BIA outputs are available. The strategy must satisfy MTD, RTO, and RPO requirements at minimum cost. Strategy categories include:

  • Recovery site strategies — where operations physically run during recovery
  • Data backup and replication strategies — how data currency is maintained
  • Personnel strategies — cross-training, alternate staff, remote work capability
  • Process workaround strategies — manual fallback procedures when systems are unavailable
  • Supplier redundancy strategies — alternate vendors for critical supplies and services

The exam often uses reciprocal agreements as a tempting-but-wrong answer when reliability is required.

5. Preventive Controls in BCP

The cheapest recovery is the one you never have to use. BCP strategy includes preventive controls designed to reduce the probability or impact of disruptions:

  • Redundant systems — RAID, clustering, load-balanced servers; eliminates single points of failure
  • Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) — maintains power during brief outages; provides time for generator startup
  • Generator backup — sustains operations during extended power failures
  • Geographic redundancy — primary and secondary data centres in different risk zones (different utility grids, different flood plains, different seismic zones)
  • Offsite backup storage — backups stored at a physically separate location (minimum 50 miles / 80 km from primary site per common practice)
  • Software escrow — source code deposited with a trusted escrow agent; released to customer if vendor fails

6. Vital Records Program

Vital Records: Documents and data essential to the organisation's legal standing, operational continuity, and recovery capability. They must be identified, classified, and protected with their own continuity plan.

Vital records categories:

  • Critical operational records — current customer accounts, financial transactions, employee records
  • Legal and governance records — incorporation documents, contracts, licences, regulatory filings
  • Recovery procedure documents — the BCP itself, DRP runbooks, system configuration documentation
  • Historical/reference records — tax records, audit trails, litigation materials
Key Concept — BCP Must Protect Itself: The BCP document is itself a vital record. If the disaster destroys the only copy of the BCP, you cannot execute it. BCP documents must be stored offsite, in multiple copies, accessible to authorised personnel without access to the primary site. Many organisations maintain printed copies at designated personnel's homes.

7. Crisis Communications Plan

The crisis communications plan defines how the organisation communicates during and after a disaster with all stakeholders. Failing to communicate promptly and accurately causes secondary crises (rumour, panic, regulatory intervention, media misreporting).

Communication Audiences

EmployeesImmediateSafety status, reporting instructions, work arrangements, system status

Major CustomersWithin hoursService impact, expected restoration timeline, workaround options
Regulators / AuthoritiesPer regulatory requirementsBreach notification (if applicable), compliance status, response measures
Media / PublicControlled, per legal adviceSingle spokesperson; factual; no speculation; regular updates

✅ Exam Tip: The CISSP consistently tests that crisis communications must be honest and timely. Withholding or delaying communication is almost never the correct answer. "Await full investigation before notifying anyone" is typically wrong — pre-notification actions and stakeholder protection begin immediately.

8. BCP Documentation Components

A complete BCP contains several component plans, each addressing different aspects of continuity:

Plan ComponentPurpose
Business Continuity Plan (BCP)Master plan for sustaining business operations during disruption; coordinates all sub-plans
Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)IT-focused plan for restoring technology infrastructure and applications
Crisis Communications PlanDefines stakeholder notification, spokesperson strategy, media management
Occupant Emergency Plan (OEP)Immediate response to physical threats: evacuation, shelter-in-place, emergency contacts
Continuity of Operations Plan (COOP)Government/military term for BCP; defines how critical government functions continue during emergencies
Cyber Incident Response Plan (IRP)Specific to cybersecurity incidents; coordinates detection, containment, eradication, recovery

Maintenance Triggers: The BCP must be reviewed and updated whenever: (1) significant organisational changes occur (acquisitions, new products, restructuring); (2) infrastructure changes (new systems, cloud migrations); (3) a real incident reveals gaps; (4) annual scheduled review; (5) results of a BCP test reveal deficiencies.

10. Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🧠 Recovery Site Temperatures: HOT→WARM→COLD = READY→SOON→LATER
Hot = Ready now (minutes/hours) — expensive
Warm = Soon (hours/days) — medium cost
Cold = Later (days/weeks) — cheap but slow
Temperature matches urgency: hotter = faster = more expensive.
🧠 Plan Components — "BCDCO−I":
BCP (master plan) → DRP (IT recovery) → Crisis Comms → OEP (building emergency) → COOP (government ops) → IRP (cyber incidents)
"Best Done Carefully — Otherwise Costly Indeed"
🧠 BCP Testing Ladder (low to high risk):
Checklist → Tabletop → Simulation → Parallel → Full Interruption
"Can This Stop Production? Full!"
⚠️ Exam Trap — Hot Site ≠ Always Correct: Hot sites are expensive. If the business's MTD is 72 hours, a cold site may be sufficient and more cost-effective. The correct recovery strategy is the CHEAPEST one that still satisfies the MTD, RTO, and RPO requirements. Don't automatically choose hot site just because it sounds more secure.
