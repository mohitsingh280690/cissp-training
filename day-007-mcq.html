Domain 1 – Security and Risk Management

1A CISSP-certified security manager discovers that her employer is systematically violating user privacy laws by selling customer data without consent — a clearly illegal practice. Her employer instructs her to remain silent. Under the (ISC)² Code of Ethics, what is her MOST appropriate course of action?
✅ Correct: C. The (ISC)² Code of Ethics Canons are prioritised in order: Canon I (Protect society, common good, and public trust) takes absolute precedence. When employer instructions conflict with societal protection and legality, the security professional must give priority to the higher canon. Illegal activity that harms society cannot be justified by employer loyalty (Canon III). Unilateral disclosure to customers (D) bypasses the proper reporting channels and could itself create issues; the correct path is reporting to appropriate authorities.
2Which sequence correctly represents the four priority-ordered Canons of the (ISC)² Code of Ethics?
✅ Correct: A. The four (ISC)² Code of Ethics Canons in priority order are: (I) Protect society, the common good, necessary public trust and confidence, and the infrastructure; (II) Act honorably, honestly, justly, responsibly, and legally; (III) Provide diligent and competent service to principals; (IV) Advance and protect the profession. The priority ordering matters — when canons conflict, lower-numbered canons take precedence. Mnemonic: "Society-Honorably-Competent-Advance" (SHCA).
3A CISSP practitioner is employed by a managed security services provider. A client asks the practitioner to conduct a penetration test on a third party's network, claiming it is a subsidiary. The practitioner cannot verify this claim and has no written authorization from the target organisation. The employer pressures the practitioner to proceed. What does ethical conduct require?
✅ Correct: B. Penetration testing without proper authorisation from the system/network owner is unauthorised access — a crime under computer abuse statutes (CFAA in the US). Canon II requires acting legally. Employer instructions do not override the legal and ethical obligation to obtain verifiable written authorisation ("get-out-of-jail letter") before any offensive security activity. A verbal claim that a target is a subsidiary, without verification, is insufficient. This is one of the clearest ethics scenarios in CISSP: no written authorisation = no test.
4RFC 1087 ("Ethics and the Internet") describes uses of the internet considered unethical. Which of the following is NOT explicitly listed as unethical in RFC 1087?
✅ Correct: C. RFC 1087 (1989) was written when the internet was a non-commercial academic/research network. It lists as unethical: seeking unauthorised access, disrupting intended use, wasting resources, destroying information integrity, and compromising user privacy. Commercial use of the internet is NOT listed as unethical in RFC 1087 — it simply wasn't the intended use at the time. Today, commercial use is entirely normalised. CISSP candidates sometimes confuse RFC 1087 with ARPANET's acceptable use policies that did restrict commercial use.
5A CISSP professional justifies a security decision by arguing: "This control will cause minor inconvenience to 1,000 employees but will prevent potential harm to 50,000 customers — the greatest good for the greatest number." Which ethical framework does this reasoning reflect?
✅ Correct: C. Consequentialism (Utilitarianism, associated with Bentham and Mill) evaluates ethical decisions by their outcomes — "the greatest good for the greatest number." Security risk management is often grounded in utilitarian calculation (weigh costs vs. benefits to affected stakeholders). Deontology (A) focuses on rules and duties regardless of outcomes (Kant's categorical imperative). Virtue ethics (B) asks what a person of good character would do. CISSP examinees should recognise these frameworks and understand that risk-based security decisions align most closely with consequentialist thinking.
6The Computer Ethics Institute's Ten Commandments of Computer Ethics begins with: "Thou shalt not use a computer to harm other people." This first commandment MOST directly addresses which type of unethical act?
✅ Correct: A. The first commandment establishes the foundational principle against intentional harm. It covers direct harm (deploying malware, launching attacks, committing fraud, cyberstalking) as well as indirect harm (writing code that facilitates criminal activity). Negligent harm (B) is covered by other professional standards. The Ten Commandments were written in 1992 and remain part of the CISSP ethics curriculum as a historical reference framework alongside the (ISC)² Code and RFC 1087.
7A security researcher discovers a critical zero-day vulnerability in widely-deployed industrial control system (ICS) firmware. They plan to publish full exploit code immediately on a public forum without notifying the vendor. Under responsible disclosure principles, what is the MOST ethical approach?
✅ Correct: C. Responsible (coordinated) disclosure is the industry-standard ethical approach: notify the vendor privately → allow a reasonable remediation period → coordinate public disclosure. Most vulnerability programs use a 90-day window (e.g., Google Project Zero). Publishing full exploit code immediately (A) is "full disclosure" — which maximises risk to unpatched systems and is generally considered irresponsible for critical infrastructure vulnerabilities. CISSPs must understand the spectrum from full disclosure to coordinated disclosure to vendor-only disclosure, and apply judgement based on the risk and vendor responsiveness.
8A CISSP professional volunteers to peer-review a colleague's published research paper, helps mentor junior security analysts, and contributes to (ISC)²'s CPE authoring program. These activities MOST directly fulfil which Canon of the (ISC)² Code of Ethics?
✅ Correct: C. Canon IV (Advance and protect the profession) is fulfilled through activities that grow and strengthen the security field: mentoring newcomers, publishing research, contributing to standards, peer review, and participation in professional organisations. This Canon is intentionally last in priority (fourth) because it benefits the profession as a whole rather than a specific principal or society directly — but it is still mandatory. Canon III (D) refers to competent service to your clients/employer — the people you directly serve.
9A CISSP who works as a CISO discovers credible evidence of financial fraud by a senior executive involving the company's cybersecurity budget. After raising the issue internally through the audit committee and board, the issue is suppressed rather than investigated. What is the MOST ethically appropriate next step?
✅ Correct: C. The (ISC)² Code requires exhausting internal channels first — which was done. When internal channels fail to address illegal activity, Canon I (protect society and public trust) requires reporting to appropriate external authorities — regulators, law enforcement, or government agencies with jurisdiction. This is the ethical escalation path. Media leaks (D) are a last resort that bypasses proper channels and may violate confidentiality obligations. Resignation without reporting (B) abandons the ethical obligation. Accepting the suppression (A) makes the CISSP complicit.
10A CISSP's certification is set to expire. She has been very busy and did not complete her required 120 Continuing Professional Education (CPE) credits over the three-year cycle. She is considering backdating some CPE records. Which statement MOST accurately describes the seriousness of this act under (ISC)² standards?
✅ Correct: B. The (ISC)² Code of Ethics applies at all times — not just in a work context (eliminating D). Falsifying CPE records is dishonest conduct in direct violation of Canon II ("act honorably, honestly, justly"). (ISC)² can and does revoke certifications for ethical violations, including falsification of CPE records. The correct action is to acknowledge the shortfall, pay the reinstatement fee if required, and complete outstanding requirements honestly. The Code applies to ALL certified members across ALL contexts — personal, professional, and online.
